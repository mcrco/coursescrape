Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

Great course. The homework can be a little bit tedious to complete. They are sometimes longer than necessary to learn the material. But they do teach you a lot and are useful for the final project.

Final project is just getting to do some research using the 200 inch telescope at Palomar with a class trip there for a weekend to collect the data for yourself. Overall, super rewarding and very fun class, and I would highly recommend anyone who needs an elective to take it.
I took this class in my last term at Caltech and it was an INCREDIBLE experience. I will remember it forever. I am so glad I took the leap to take this class. I would recommend it highly to anyone looking for a science requirement that will be fulfilling.

At many points in the course, I felt very discouraged because it is nearly impossible to complete any of these assignments on your own. But the amount and quality of support available at office hours is truly amazing. Zack might be the greatest TA to ever exist, period. He makes sure you understand every single thing and helps you through the babiest of questions. He also offers up a LOT of extra time and office hours. I would have completed a total of 0 assignments without his help. I have learned a lot during his office hours. Please, please, please keep hiring Zack as the TA for this class.

The assignments can be gruellingly long (but there are not that many of them). Start early!

I view the project and trip to Palomar as the capstone experience of my degree and a quintessential Caltech experience. I could not recommend it more. Palomar, in the way that you get to experience it through this class as "observers", is a peaceful and serene intellectual paradise. This is a trip is a core memory for me and I would not miss it for the world. The opportunity to make observations on a real-life large telescope as a non-astronomy major is a once-in-a-lifetime experience. I value it immensely. I will remember it fondly.

Another learning from this class was learning how to build, set-up, and use amateur small telescopes. After this class, you have permission to check out telescopes from Cahill and take them on observation trips with your friends! I am glad to have picked up this skill.

If you have some time and need the science credits (or even if you don't need the science credits), I cannot recommend this class enough.
