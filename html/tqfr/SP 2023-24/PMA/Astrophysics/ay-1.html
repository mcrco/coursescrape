Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

As an freshman applied physics major, I found Ay 1 to be an great menu course. We covered units (parsecs, arcseconds, etc.), astronomical coordinate systems (right ascension, declination, etc.), Kepler's laws, electromagnetic waves, apparent/absolute magnitude, HR diagrams, galaxy formation, dark matter/energy, cosmological constant. There is no real textbook to the course, just Monday Wednesday lecture (Friday is recitation) and about 1 problem set per week (a few weeks had none). The problem sets are light (1-3 hrs). Lecture is interesting but there is a lot of information to process, so it is hard to stay focused sometimes. There is a closed notes midterm (1 page of notes), and the final is a 3 page paper on an astro seminar video (easy). Towards the end of the term, there was a field trip to Palomar Observatory, which was really fun and interesting (wear *multiple* layers if you go, its very cold). I found the course content to be very interesting (the Hubble Tension?), and coursework to be light (easy to get A also). I would highly recommend taking Ay 1 as a menu class (over EE 1 even).
