I was over the fence between taking this class and taking the analytical track initially (as someone who took Ma1b anal - which I highly recommend for everyone) - and I am glad I took this class. While I did take multivariate calculus in high school, here there was a greater emphasis on the vector calculus approach (as you have taken linear algebra before, compared to a lot of the case in high school where linear algebra comes after).

Hence, a lot of the notation was fairly intuitive, and the textbook was a godsend. Read Marsden ‼️ and do the examples for practice. The examples in Marsden + the problems at the back are quite difficult computationally, but they prepare you well to do the sets and the exams.

I did not really need to go to office hours for this class, which was a change compared to Ma1a and Ma1b anal. Simply read the textbook, do practice problems, and go to recitation. I worked on most of the sets alone, but there were some confusing problems on the last 3 sets - which I worked with friends on. Check your sets with a set study group, and explain concepts with each other.

This is a great balance between a computational and slightly analytical background. The course was fairly reasonable, and I think it is definitely fairly trivial to do well in.
