Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

Great class, especially if you're interested in the material. Lectures are very easy to follow, and there's super clear lecture notes. There are 9 sets throughout the term, each of which I thought was around the expected/reasonable workload every week.
If you are taking this class over other logic classes or at all, it is most likely required. While it was annoying occasionally, it was fairly manageable with no background except solid proofreading skills (more on that later).

Structure: There are only 9 problem sets with no exams, with one non-collab question per set. This non-collab problem was more often than not on the harder side, which made it particularly difficult to get help for in Monday night as you try to crank it out (the sets are due Tuesday at 1 pm). The sets get more difficult as the term goes on, peaking at Set 7/8, but in general are ok. There was a group of four of us who would be able to finish the set in around 6 - 8 hours, including reading the notes, brainstorming, etc.

Style: There were consistently a couple of problems per set that were fairly difficult to get started, as the idea almost always involved some clever construction motivated by the notes or from just thin air. It was certainly doable with enough time and playing around, but as mentioned, solid proof-writing and just general exposure to pure math topics would save you a lot of stress.

TA: I think Michael was a phenomenal TA by all accounts and is what made this class a lot more tolerable than I was expecting. He was always available via email, and often responded with much more information than I was expecting, almost always giving me enough to finish the problem on my own while not giving away the solution by any means. During his office hours, he would take the time to connect the topics in the class to ideas that might be more familiar, which made the topics and notation a lot less daunting.

Overall, this class is doable with previous exposure to pure math topics (i.e. induction, graph theory, etc), but can be annoying from time to time with the weekly assignments and non-collab. This was countered by Michael being a great TA, and overall made this class a lot less painful.
