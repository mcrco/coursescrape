Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

Very good intro lab, especially if you are in the first two terms and can take it on pass-fail. If you take it on pf, there are no hw requirements.
Class is overall pretty chill. Each week, the homework consists of Taylor and Mathematica. The Mathematica HW takes around 15 minutes and Taylor takes around 30 minutes, so really light workload. The lab itself is done all in class, and is also really simple. The instructions are really clear and you just follow all the steps, and the TAs are there to help out if you ever get slightly confused. Just write down everything into the lab notebook and they dish out 100s. Overall really chill class, not very interesting but good for the easy A for the lab core requirement (the only neg is to get an A+ is you have to go "above and beyond", which no one really knows what that means).
I genuinely really enjoyed this class. It is very easy to follow. The Taylor assignments were sometimes a bit confusing but you get to fix any minor mistakes in lab. I think the lab portion was really well designed because the lab instructions are explained extremely well. The TAs were helpful and nice if you had any questions. The professor is really nice, enthusiastic, and understanding. Do submit any Taylor corrections you have because these will bring your grade down drastically. Even if it is already past the due date, try to fix those before the class ends.
This was a great intro lab course! You learn the practical application of the physics covered in Ph 1, which is a really interesting connection. The outside assignments - Mathematica and Taylor problem sets - are short and (somewhat) simple and you get to correct them during the lab period. This is a wonderful, low-stress class to learn about physics, so I would recommend it!
Highly recommend taking this course for your additional intro lab! It's very chill and fun. If you are taking it on grades, do the HW, but don't stress too much about it since you can correct mistakes in class after it is graded and get full credit on it. Find a partner/partners to do the labs with-- it makes it more fun and the lab gets done quicker! TAs were all very nice, so ask questions if you need help. Also, Professor Black explains the physics behind the labs very well, so be sure to ask him about that as well. Overall, the class was great; I enjoyed it and it is not very time-consuming, except for the Taylor HW (every week there is one set of Mathematica problems and one set of Taylor HW. Mathematica is very chill)
Easiest intro lab requirement class. Just show up and do the labs and you'll pass, satisfying the lab requirement.
If you need to take a lab, Ph3 is reasonable. Lab does take the entire time period if you want to do a good job and have time to fix your homework (you are always given your graded homework back during lab period and then have the opportunity to fix mistakes to get a 100). If you take the class on grades, it's very possible to get an A, you just need to put the time (this is a correctly united class) and take the care to write your notebook well.
Put in the extra effort and take this course on grades for the A. If you don't want to do that then don't.
Pretty chill class. I took it on grades as a Junior and ended up doing fine, but the homework can be tiresome to do when you're no longer a frosh. It's a lot of stats/sig figs and not much physics, so it is doable even if its been a while since physics core. You don't need to do any homework if you take the class on pf.

Labs were pretty fun! I enjoyed the experience, and while I'm not too sure how much I learned, I had fun.
