Take this. Don't take SEC11
If you're taking SEC 13, it's probably because it's required. So, I'll try to help distinguish it from the other SEC choices.

SEC 13 has weekly homework which consists usually of: (1) reading ~20 pages from a book, (2) answering questions about the reading, and (3) drafting a short (usually <1 page) piece of writing. In different weeks, the drafts included an elevator pitch, a pitch deck, a one-pager, and a pop-sci article. Usually, this took me more than the allotted 2 hours, but not a great deal more. There was no midterm or final, nor any "cumulative" final writing assignment, which did take the stress off.

Class is a lecture, not a discussion, and you're expected to sign in each day. You can miss at most one lecture; after that, missing subsequent lectures requires you to go through the lecture slides and do all of the activities yourself (this is a lot more work than attending lecture is).

It definitely makes more sense to take this class as a senior than as an underclassman, but ideally don't take it third term so you don't have to worry about graduating (assuming you can manage that on Glitch Day!).

The class is based on a set of surveys of alumni about what kind of class they would've found valuable at Caltech, and indeed, it is fairly targeted towards Caltech students as an audience. I feel like I learned some reasonable skills and -- as far as required writing classes go -- this was a pretty solid one. The prof is kind and interesting!

(As a heads-up, some of the readings are a bit outdated, so if you're sensitive to somewhat uncritical portrayals of history, either brace yourself or consider a different SEC writing class.)
