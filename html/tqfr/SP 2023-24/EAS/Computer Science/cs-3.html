Definitely expect to put a lot of work into this class, but also expect to get a lot out of it. I have had a very good experience in CS 3---the lectures in particular are fun and interesting. If you are specifically worried about not having prior experience with C, I would recommend taking this class anyway if you are otherwise interested. No experience with C is assumed, and as someone who went in without any experience in C, I am very happy with how far I have come with C in the past ten weeks.
It's a great crash course into C. I think you could learn C online as well, although perhaps not as quickly.
The course has now been restructured. You will have solo projects, group projects, and in-class quizzes (diagnostics) where you will either work alone or in a group. All these, combined with the game, will determine your overall grade. Make sure you start the assignments earlier because sometimes you will have a group project and a solo project due in the same week. In the last three weeks of the course, you will build a game with your selected group (three people per group). I found this part interesting and rewarding to see our game working at the end. The class is taught in C; there is a lot of coding and it takes a lot of time. So be prepared for that!
Get together a good group before the course starts and make sure you are all in the same lab section. My experience was alright because I did this, but I heard of many other groups who had members drop or just didn't work well together. Overall, CS 3 was alright. It was not as interesting as CS 2 content-wise but was definitely a lot more work. Office hours were an absolute mess, often with one TA present and 15+ students waiting to enqueue and so the queue would close immediately and you'd have to wait for a long time to get helped. Coming from CS 2, it was nice to get some experience writing programs where you could actually see the result as opposed to data structures. However, the experience was not as enjoyable in my opinion, and expect to dedicate a decent amount of time to this course especially toward the last few weeks when you're working on the game.
This class, in theory, should be fascinating. you create both a game and a web server to host the game on, which is pretty cool. There are, however, some implementation issues. The projects tend to have bugs in them, which makes it difficult to tell whether you messed up or whether the code that you were given had bugs in it already. Office hours, while helpful, are simply too difficult to attend, as the queue will fill up for an hour and a half block after just five minutes. Overall, the class is very interesting, but issues with course organization/not double-checking assignments before releasing them and office hours make this course difficult for reasons beyond a student's control.

This course was very well structured in teaching C, even to someone who has never seen the material before. I found that the content built well on itself each week, and it was easy to follow and understand with plenty of in-lecture examples. The in-class diagnostics were helpful for solidifying information, asking questions was easy with all the in-lecture TAs being available consistently. The weekly labs made it easy to understand the course material and get direct feedback on assignments, as well as clear up content misunderstandings, understand your grade, and improve for the next assignment. Make sure to work early on your assignments and go to office hours early with bugs, and you will do great. Also make sure to take advantage of lab feedback and incorporate it next week.
This course was definitely worth taking and improved my software development skills. Not an easy course, but you will learn a lot. Code quality is emphasized so make sure to adhere strictly to the guidelines. Also, solo assignments were worth 40% (with 4 solo assignments total = 10% each), so don't take these lightly. Take if you are considering SWE.
