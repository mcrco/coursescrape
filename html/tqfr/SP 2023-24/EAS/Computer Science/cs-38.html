Pros of CS 38: The course content is something that I should learn as a CS major, and this class made me learn it
Cons of CS 38: I think everything else

Lectures
- I don't know if this is the common experience, but I could not handle going to lectures in this class. I usually go to every lecture for my classes because I find it helpful at the minimum to at least be in an environment where I can follow the notes and ask questions when needed. I forced myself to go to the first 4-5 lectures where the professor would face the board and copy the notes word for word, barely audible. I don't know if the lectures improved after that because it was actively distracting to be in the room. Sorry.
- The lecture NOTES (and TA notes) on the other hand were very well organized. Sometimes they wouldn't really explain some things but between the two sets of provided notes, it was enough to learn the material mostly just off of the notes.

GRADING
- The grading in CS 38 is some of the most inconsistent grading I've seen. I've seen many students where one sentence would be phrased incorrectly, and over 50% of the problem is marked wrong when clearly they understood the problem. Then in the next set, they would mark off the exact same type of error only 16%.
- If your answer is different from the TA's expected answers, they would just mark a lot of it wrong. They would then take a month to answer your regrade request and then finally give the points back.
- This class has a weird rule where you have 3 cumulative regrade requests, but a successful regrade request doesn't count. They say the point of this is to prevent people from spamming regrade requests, but this rule is really dumb because it just made me not want to regrade request legitimate claims because the TAs would frequently reject requests for other students.
- The grading is over a month late and it makes it really hard to figure out when I should actually use the regrade requests, and when I should be using my drop problems. This is not ok.

The sets were bit difficult but honestly it was fine, I don't have much complaints. My number one complaint by far is the grading in this class. And the lectures. I had to basically just teach myself. Overall, I would not recommend this class (but if you're taking this class, you probably didn't have a choice anyways).
If you don't have to take it to graduate, don't. This class is ONLY useful for students going to grad school. If you are going to the industry, practicing leetcode questions and doing algorithm related personal projects are way more useful. In fact, algorithm-adjacent courses, such as CS21 and CS13 are far more useful than this class for understanding algorithms.
