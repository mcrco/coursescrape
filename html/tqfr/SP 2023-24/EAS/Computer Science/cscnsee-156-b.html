Start earlier than you think you should and make sure you have a good group otherwise the work will fall on you. Also, a majority of your grade is based on your leaderboard placement, so really try to focus on this. HPC shut down for us during finals week and throughout the term (especially near the end), so try to finish the work at the start because even though it was "resolved" some students did not have access near the end.
In CS 156b, you don't have to put a ton of focus into the course, but make sure you have a team that communicates and is responsive. Split work around and be ready for presentations every three weeks. If you do that, it's not much work and you should be able to get an A.
This is not an "easy A" type of class. If you want to do well in this class then find a group who don't have this mind set and instead actually want to make the most out of the class - because what you put in is what you get out. You could build a really cool model that you are proud of. Be aware that in most groups there are only 1 or 2 people who do 95% of the work. Don't just make a group with your friends if they don't agree with you on how much you will care about the class.
Leaderboard heavily affects grade, even though they may say it's only a minor portion. Other assignments weren't even graded lol.
Don't be discouraged if what you try does not work, the TAs and instructor just want to see that you TRIED. So try a couple different models, preprocessing techniques, etc and then you will have plenty to write about for your individual reports.
I really liked this course and enjoyed the fact that we built a whole machine learning pipeline
