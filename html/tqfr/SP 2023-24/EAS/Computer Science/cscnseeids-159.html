Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

Topic is interesting, I hope they make the presentation-style more engaging though.
You get out what you put into the class, it was all just one big project that you have complete freedom in choosing so unless you have a very exciting project idea and want to put in a significant amount of effort, you might not learn much.
This year's topic for CS 159 was "LLMs for Reasoning," meaning that we essentially had to utilize an existing LLM and apply it to a problem space requiring some semblance of reasoning (coding, math, games, data analysis, etc.), and apply some optimizations/techniques to make it perform better than the LLM could've on its own. The class had a lot of interesting guest speakers and gave you a lot of ideas to work with in terms of approaches/frameworks for thinking about the project, which was very nice. The grade is composed of two assignments: an in-class group presentation where you present a seminal work within the realm of LLMs for reasoning (10% of the grade) and a final project consisting of a proposal turned in around a month into the term and a final writeup turned in at the end of the term (90% of the grade). Due to the nature of this year's topic, it was relatively easy to start the project 2-3 weeks before the end of the term and crank it all out without too much stress. I would recommend putting in a decent amount of thought into your proposal, as it makes executing the project much easier and less of a scramble. The TAs provide helpful feedback and are available for consultation if you reach out to them, which is nice. Overall, this is a great advanced CS class which allows you to explore a very hot area of computer science with a great degree of autonomy, so I'd definitely recommend it if there's anything you want to explore within the domain of LLMs (or whatever the special topic is in the future) in a self-directed manner.
Workload is very low but make sure the topic is interesting to you and if it's not, skip the class. Yisong is a good lecturer but he only lectured for the first part of the class, then it was student presentations.
