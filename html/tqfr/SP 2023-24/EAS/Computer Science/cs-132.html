What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

This is a really fun and interesting course. It's just that sometimes it's extremely unorganized, which would be ok if we didn't have at least 4 other classes to deal with. Overall worth the knowledge that was gained but could definitely be improved upon
very fun
Honestly, you might be better off learning web dev on your own. The class is poorly structured and organized as all El classes are. Grading takes the entire term, and you never really know what is going on logistically with the assignments since everything is always changing. I don't think learning web dev in this class is worth the stress of it.
You'll learn web development (no front-end frameworks like React or Angular though). Don't expect much feedback on your work though. Grading seems to be a bit late. But do go to office hours, especially El's, and you'll definitely get out of the class what you need.
CS 132 provides a lot of foundational knowledge for HTML, CSS, and JavaScript and I didn't know anything about any of them before taking it. It was a lot of material to cover, but I think it was okay for me. I would say that the content is very important for CS majors to learn to be able to apply to any kind of software, but the class is just not taught well. It's not too much work week-by-week but the final project is pretty long.
tl;dr I have some grievances with the class, but you will learn a lot and the projects can be fun. I would recommend this class to anyone wanting to learn about webdev (or general fullstack practices). I would not invest a lot of effort into it because the flaws with the course are very frustrating and the assignments can take a long time if you try to be perfectionistic about it.

The good: Hovik is very knowledgeable about this subject and cares a lot about their students. The assignments are pretty relevant practices of what you're learning. If this is a topic you're interested in, you will learn a lot. The creative project assignments are pretty fun.

The bad:
- Hovik used a Discord to communicate info. They pinged "@everyone" 132 times over the course of the term. There are 74 days between classes starting and commencement. This made it hard to find relevant information, which was an issue when homework specs would be updated after release.
- The slides are good, but the search feature and the convert to PDF button only work intermittently, making it hard to find the relevant info.
- There was no feedback on any homeworks until ~May 18th (the first one was due April 13).
- Hovik constantly begged us to come to OH, and would emphasize that assignments can't be done in a day early in the week--which made me avoid OH and assignments. It's the same feeling as when my mom talks to me about everything she has to do; I think they were (unintentionally) trying to offload their stress onto the students. it felt like they didn't trust that we'd do the work, and I would go as far as saying they care too much about their students.
- Their policy regarding online resources was confusing. W3 schools and maybe stack overflow (?) were banned because they might have outdated information or bad practices. I understand warning against using them, but 1) the job of a software engineer is to navigate online documentation and advice with critical thinking; it would be nice if we could be trusted, as CS majors, to develop this skill for ourselves and 2) you could just... take off points for bad practice instead of trying to state rules (???) in the hard to navigate discord.
