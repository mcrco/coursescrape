I have mixed feelings about this course. I will try to open up both strengths and weaknesses objectively. One of the biggest strengths of the class are lectures and slides. Georgia's lecturing skills are exceptional. She is a great lecturer! She was able to clearly explain difficult concepts by slowly building advanced concepts on top of the easier concepts. She was able to maintain students' engagement and focus by the use of visual aids and verbal explanations. I felt like she digested the content so well before presenting it to students which made it so clear and easy to understand. She asked several times to make sure students followed and emphasized when to pay extra attention. Another strength of the class is the content which is interesting and state-of-the-art. The third strength of the class is the course assignments that include coding assignments and paper readings. Although it was a lot, they were designed to develop a deep foundational understanding of large language and vision models. Combining these strength, you can learn a lot of up-to-date material that is extremely useful and helpful for anyone interested in the subject.

One of the biggest weaknesses of the course is that it did not provide a safe environment to learn. It was more of a judgemental space where students were not encouraged to ask questions or actively engage with the course instructors. Georgia's tone sometimes was passive-aggressive both during lectures and in Piazza comments. TAs skipped some of the questions that students asked on Piazza without answering them. TAs also sometimes responded on Piazza passively-aggressively. There were several questions from different students that TAs would answer with a generic copied and pasted sentence from their previous response to another student. For some questions, TAs responded with generic vague answers that were not helpful. There were similar situations in TA office hours. I came with the intention to learn and asked if my understanding of how to answer one of the questions is correct. TA would say yes to the answer that later turned out to be wrong. Another TA responded to my question "Please ask a different TA". It is totally fine when TA does not know the answer but this situation showed no effort from the TA to help me. I would expect them to ask other TAs themselves and follow up with me later. I would be comfortable with just a little more engagement and effort from that TA. One of the students asked for more TA office hours on Piazza. Georgia responded that they won't add any additional TA office hours because "TAs were already working at full capacity" which I don't think is an appropriate response to the student who is trying to learn. The syllabus did not contain the time for instructors' office hours, only TAs. The instructors were not readily available for questions. For the final project, the course instructors posted the grades without any comments on why substantial parts of points were docked. The absence of any feedback on this assignment deprives the students from the possibility of learning from their mistakes. Another weakness of the course is that it is too time-consuming. In some cases unnecessarily. For example, the last assignment was too time-consuming where students struggled just to get through instead of actually learning. I think implementing GANs or VAEs in the last assignment would have been so much more helpful.

Overall, it is a great course where you can learn a lot but it is extremely time-consuming. Also, the overall attitude of course instructors was arrogant, passive-aggressive and scarce on feedback.

The class seemed to be significantly better than last year. The problem sets were difficult and time consuming, but also pretty enjoyable and interesting. The lectures are very well done and definitely worth your time. The course covers a lot of material, starting from simple MLPs, then looking at basic models like ResNet and VITS, then covering LLMs, and finally diffusion models. Because there is so much content, make sure to dedicate time to understanding everything because it is very easy to just speed past it. You have four late days, save most for that final set. It has a lot of math that isn't too horrible, but does take time to go through. You will also be required to write a project proposal for some novel research question that relates to material learned in class. Don't stress too much about this! They seemed relatively nice with grading. The content is definitely worth it- I've walked away from this class with much more knowledge, understanding, and interest in these advanced models.

The only major negative with this class is that the course staff and professors are unhelpful with problem set questions. Once again, this seems to be improved from last year as the atmosphere was generally welcoming, but it's still difficult to get help when needed. If you have conceptual questions about the material, feel free to ask. But in OH or on Piazza, questions are usually met with brief one sentence answers that often just restate the question. Take the class with some friends, or make a study group to check answers with. It'll make the class much easier.

This is the most content rich, educational machine learning class I have taken at Caltech. The assignments are challenging but educationally valuable. I would recommend it to anyone seriously interested in Machine Learning.

However, if you do decide to take this class out of interest, do not expect an easy class. The assignments are 2 weeks long, and you should not start them in the last week. If you start the assignments on time then you will get a lot of value out of them without a lot of stress.

The infamous assignment 4 is very challenging, because if you are the average Caltech undergraduate you will, like me, probably not have a sufficient math background to be able to easily breeze through it. I recommend starting very early, spending lots of time on it, going to office hours, and reading lots of appendices from all the relevant papers as there will be lots of relevant information in there.

The lectures (especially Georgia's) are some of the most well structured, clear lectures I have seen on foundational topics in ML (eg: the lectures on transformers/attention), and I highly recommend attending them.

Lastly, the class did not give out any A+s this year, if that is something you care about.
