Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

A delightfully chill EE core class after everything you go through prior. Even more chill if you take 10 as a frosh. I like the transistor analysis you learn in the course. It's honestly super cool especially as they are very important components to learn how to work with. It's definitely the slowest paced EE core class I've taken. Shawn is also a great TA and the labs are very chill I think they took like 3 hours if you have prior experience. I would do sets when they come out to make your term chill.
great course! the workload gets more manageable after the first 5 weeks, since you no longer have to submit a weekly lab and homework assignment. Instead, you begin working on your project and continue with the weekly homeworks. keep in mind though, that you still need to submit weekly progress reports on the project.
After taking EE44, EE45 is a much easier class even though it's rated at 12 units. If you are a sophomore and somehow took EE113 the previous winter, the 45 labs will become every much easier too. For about 7 weeks, there's a weekly homework and a lab that are both due the morning before lecture. Lectures were at 9AM this year and fairly good as Professor Emami covers everything that's asked in homeworks and the midterm. I personally didn't get to go to lecture as much, but the provided book (Sedra Smith) is a great textbook and contains all the information necessray to do well in the class, and sometimes even arguably better than lecture. There is a final project due at the end of the term that is also not too challenging but a fun experience if you haven't built many circuits.

One thing I hope can be improved is the labs, as I felt most of the work was just busy work since we had to copy the procedures basically 1:1 as we did it. If you missed just even a small step/data to report out of the 20 steps, you lose 5-10 points which I think is a bit unreasonable. The actual circuit building and debugging process never took me more than 30 minutes, but trying to follow the steps carefully since the lab book is a bit hard to read and the required data is not clear.
EE 45 is an amazing class! It re-sparked my interest in hardware that had got beaten down in 44; this class was so healing! The sets were very effective in guiding you through new concepts in way that would lead to long term learning. The labs took a while sometimes, but the TAs were great, and the labs teach you so so much. Professor Emami is amazing and extremely compassionate towards students. The class is properly united, but the class is enjoyable. If you do the labs and start the sets early so you can get help if needed, you can absolutely get a good grade in the class and learn a lot.
Very nice class, I think the 12 units is fair unlike 44 and 55. Professor Emami is a great teacher so try to go to her lectures if possible, but if you miss them then the textbook is actually pretty good. The labs for the first half are relatively chill but can take a while so it's good to start them early. The quizzes are usually only in the second half, though I think it varies from year to year - it's also not clear what the weighting of those vs the midterm is (the syllabus says 25% total is midterm and quizzes), but the quizzes were very chill and the midterm is representative of the content so it's not necessarily easy but it's definitely something you should be prepared for. At least this year, professor Emami held an office hour every week - it's very chill and an extra hour to ask any lecture questions (it was at a time not so convenient for homework questions) so that is a resource that you can be aware of bc I wasn't when I started this class. The demo at the end of the class is also very informal - no need to stress because there's a lot of resources available to finish the projects on time. Overall a fun class and would definitely recommend even if it weren't required!
Overall, it was a good class. The homework assignments are not that bad, but I felt like the labs were a lot of busy work, and not enough experimenting. Also, the labs felt a bit canned (ie, you do this and should get this result), which is different to other lab classes such as EE113 where the goal is more broad. The lectures were pretty useful, if a bit rushed at times. The final project was pretty manageable, but again, there was not that much freedom in what we could do and I felt like it wasn't that connected to what was taught in the class (the piano project, for example, deal exclusively with op-amps and not MOSFETS, BJTs, or diodes).
I think this was a great class. The lectures were very clear, methodical, and well-paced; and I got along pretty well just going to lecture and not reading the textbook. The labs walked us step-by-step on how to use lab equipment and build small breadboard circuits. This is a 12 unit class because there are both labs and sets, both of which are not too difficult but do take some time to complete. The labs may go quicker if you have some previous experience, but if not, the TAs are very helpful for debugging.

The second half of the course doesn't have labs and is focused on building a project of your choice. Three project options are provided but you can also choose your own project if you wish. During finals week we all demo-ed our projects and ate lunch together. This class was a great time.
