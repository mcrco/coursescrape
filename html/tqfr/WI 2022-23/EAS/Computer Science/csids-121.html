You should definitely take this class if you're interested in a more practical learning approach within CS or if you want to learn SQL. There were about 7 homework assignments which were, for the most part, of an appropriate length. The assignments, lectures, and reworks were great at reinforcing learning. The final project is also great to have for future use. The class could be better organized, and I wish they had more in person OH. However, it is very clear how much Prof. Hovik cares about their students, and they are an amazing instructor.

Its a fun class, but a lot of the important knowledge that will be used in the future & industry is only the middle 3 weeks of the class, the rest seems like a filler.
This class was amazing! El is an amazing teacher and really cares about her students. She is very enthusiastic about the subject and wants to make sure students also feel that enthusiasm. All of the lectures were very well organized and connected all the topics we learned together.

I did not have much experience with databases before this class, but after taking it feel confident enough to answer questions about databases during job interviews. The final project at the end of the class tied everything we learned together and it felt good to create something of your own that you could show on your resume.

The class was primarily organized on Discord again this year but I think the organization improved a lot compared to last year. El did also email important announcements out for those that do not check Discord often. This class had assignments due every week and each lecture had exit tickets that you could use to get extra time on assignments. There was no midterm and instead of a final we had a final project where we made a SQL database that interfaced with a Python app on any topic of our choosing. The final project was much more rewarding than an exam and really made me understand how the material we learned all ties together when making an actual final product.

Overall, I would recommend this class for anyone, even if you feel databases aren't your thing. It is very rewarding and database theory + application is a skill that I think helps in all areas of software engineering. The class had a decent amount of work but it was all very rewarding at the end and I gained confidence in my knowledge of databases and how to work with them.
This is a great class and one of the most practical CS courses offered at Caltech. The assignments were reasonable, and the final project was fun! There was no midterm this year. El is very understanding and kind, and they are always willing to meet with you to clarify content or discuss your work. The material was presented at an appropriate pace. I will say that the class (assignments, grading, and scheduled meetings) can sometimes be a bit disorganized, but this is getting better, and El is always receptive to feedback. I highly recommend this class if you are interested in learning about databases and SQL.

I took 121 after being familiar with SQL from internships and hackathons, but I still thought it was very valuable. First, I've become extremely confident in designing databases and organizing tables, and you learn very interesting advanced features. Essentially, anything you might need to do with SQL is covered up to cursors. That's cool! The coding parts of 121 were always very fun and engaging (and not too hard).

Then comes the boring part. You learn normal forms (BCNF, 3NF). This is boring! Except people spend meetings deciding the correct normal forms for organizing their tables. So now it's cool!There was one cool lecture where you learn how data is organized and it even references ideas from CS24 like caching and how it all comes together very practically for making scalable systems. I've needed this info for interviews before! Cool! Professor has shown that they're the best lecturer at identifying what skills have large practical value.

tl;dr very practical and useful even if you are already familiar with SQL
