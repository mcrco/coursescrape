ME72 is a core experience for meches on this campus and Mello does a great job making it fun and a great learning experience. I had no robotics experience before this course and I learned so much throughout it and enjoyed it a lot. It is a lot of hours in the shop, but that's nice because not a lot of courses here give you that kind of experience and it made me a lot more comfortable in the shop. Trent is an amazing shop manager and gives the best advice and help ever, and is always super helpful and kind. The course gets stressful leading up to presentations and the competition, but that's pretty expected. You'll do fine and probably have a good experience as long as you get along with your team and schedule/plan well.
Hopefully you already know what you're getting yourself into, but get ready for a wild ride and hang on. It will be lots of fun, lots of work, lots of hours slaving away in the shop, but it is so worth it! Paul and Trent are incredible resources, ask them for help for anything, no matter if you think it might be too big or too small.
ME 72 certainly lived up to the hype. It's no secret that this course is an incredible amount of work. The sooner you just accept it the better. There will be late nights and early mornings in the shop, and at times it may be stressful, but it's all part of the experience. And now having gone through it, I don't think there are many other courses that offer as valuable of an experience for MEs. But that's just my opinion. I will have great memories of ME 72 for the rest of my life and I feel like it made me a much more experienced engineer. I will say that the team is the most important thing. Make sure you choose to work with people that you get along with and that you feel will contribute to the team. The team will make or break the experience, no doubt about it.
This class is reliant on choosing a good group. Choose your teammates wisely. You will spend a lot of time in lab weekly, so be sure to manage your other courses efficiently. Be prepared for things to go wrong. Don't stress too much. Work hard and roll with the punches. It is a lot of work, but a lot of fun! Good luck!
Here are some tips you should take note of if you're on the fence about taking ME 72:
- Is it a lot of time? Yeah, but only if you make it a lot of time. As long as you plan your time well, you can easily make ME 72 a 9-unit class.
- What if I don't have a team? Yeah, this is a pretty big problem, you should definitely find a team as soon as possible. Ask around on the first day. Do not wait. Although Mello will try to add you to the other teams, it's generally up to them whether or not to add you.
- What if I suck at machining stuff? Great! That's a really good reason to take the class. By the end of it, you should be very confident in your machining ability.
- Is it fun? Incredibly, and especially if you have a good team. Easily one of my best engineering experiences.
