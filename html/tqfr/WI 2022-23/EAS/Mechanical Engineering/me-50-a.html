Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? Lab course on solid mechanics. Topics include tensile testing, theory and use of strain gages, and some enriching demonstrations of and interferometer and laser vibrometer. Highly recommend you attend lecture and optional lab demonstrations because Mello always has something cool to show you.

After the lab portion, there is an ANSYS tutorial and two ANSYS lab assignments, where you learn how to perform static structural, transient structural, and modal analysis.

Word of caution: time management is critical in this class. A two-week lab should be expected to require two weeks of work.

Hopefully future iterations will have TAs who get their grading done on time. Still waiting for lab 2 to be graded and it's March 19. C'mon TAs...

According to the TQFRs for this class as far back as 2015-2016, students usually spend upwards of 20 hours per week on this class, and the workload hasn't changed very much over the past 7 years. The workload this year was no different.

The office hours for this class are not helpful. If you ask a TA a question about Ncorr or ANSYS, they will usually tell you they don't know the answer and to ask a student who has already done the assignment for help. If that doesn't work, they will usually tell you to create a new file and redo the entire analysis.

Only the first report was graded before the end of finals week. It is almost impossible to not lose points for formatting something slightly different from how the TAs wanted it, so having reports graded earlier would have been helpful to avoid losing points for the same formatting issues on multiple reports.

Most assignments have some errors or missing steps that prevent you from doing the assignment. Since the office hours won't help you with this, you'll have to spend many hours on each assignment scouring the Internet and asking other students to figure out some trivial setting that the assignment doesn't mention. Also, every ANSYS assignment will have some settings that have changed after the assignment was written several years ago. You will spend even more time figuring out what the names of the settings changed to.

Try to schedule your labs as late in the week as possible. Lab reports are due 1 or 2 weeks after the day you do the lab, so your report will be due later if you do the lab later. This won't give you any extra time to do the report, but by the time you start your report, Professor Mello will have covered more material in lecture and will have posted clarifications and hints for the report on Canvas. This will make it easier to write your report. If you start your report early, you will miss out on this extra information and it will take you longer to write your report.
When you take this course, please keep in mind that the 2-week assignments are meant to be done over the course of 2 weeks, not in the 2 days before (or well after) the due date.
