Hands down the best course I've taken at Caltech, both due to the content and the way it was instructed. Lectures were engaging, learning was interesting, and Professor Hunt clearly cared not just about the coursework but also about students' well-being and experience.
I find textbook examples and equation derivations very helpful supplemental material.
Melany is a really good professor who breaks down the material in a way that is easy to understand even the harder concepts. The lecture slides were useful, however a lot of the worked out examples were only written on the board, not on the slides themselves so it was helpful to go to class. The grading was fairly nice, with a 6 sets (instead of the initial 7), a in class short presentation on a thermodynamic topic of your choice, 2 quizzes, final, and attendance/participation. The textbook was useful at times, but overall the lectures were enough. Sometimes, office hours would be the saving grace to some homework questions where the formula was not given in lecture or in the textbook.

Super interesting course content. Super unfortunate final.
10/10 class! Melany cares deeply about her students and reiterates that we can ask her any questions about the content. The content pacing was on point, and the quizzes were fair. I wish the final grade was curved upwards a bit more (median of the class was B+), but otherwise the exams/hw reflected what was being lectured about. The homework could be solving using what we learned in class and/or reading the textbook (same book as ME11a), which took me about 6-9 hr outside of class. I got a lot out of in-person lectures, but the recordings were fine. I know other people had course conflicts approved, and I think they missed out on having Hunt as a wonderful lecturer. She always brought amazing demos to class. I was able to use ME 11 to substitute the thermo requirements for my MS major.
Most people taking the course will be required to (MechE's). However, I advice not taking it as a requirement, but, take it as a real learning experience because recruiters really ask about a lot of the stuff learned in this class. regarding the work and material, try your hardest to stay on top of it because it is very easy to fall behind. Attend office hours as always, and if you have any questions on past or current psets, dont hesitate to send emails with questions.
