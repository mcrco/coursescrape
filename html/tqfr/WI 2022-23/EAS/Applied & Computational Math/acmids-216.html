Great class. 4 problems sets spread out every 2 weeks or so. Sets are definitely lengthy, but I found the problems generally interesting. Workload can seem decently high during weeks with set due dates depending on how early you started the set. TAs were accessible and generally understanding/gracious about extensions. Would recommend this class to others.
4 sets, no exams. The first two sets involve a lot of bashing, the last two sets are more applied. The lectures are generally decent, although the prof pretty much maintains the same pace throughout regardless of how technical the material is. Luckily there are also pretty good lecture notes
This is an interesting course where you will learn about theory and application of Markov Chains. At times the course can be a bit dense on the theory side, and if you don't have a math or applied math background, then it can get confusing. I didn't find the derivations to be that important to the course, and they definitely were not needed for the homework. The simulation techniques are the most fun part of the course, as you get to see how you can actually see how Markov chains are used in research. There were 4 homeworks and we were given 2 weeks per homework. I found the homeworks to be fair in both content and length.
Very interesting course. Lectures can get a bit theoretical and technical, but all of the sets are reasonably doable and there is plenty of time to complete them.
Excellent material, pretty good sets. Sets are long but reasonable, and cover a good survey of modern topics regarding Markov chains and stochastic processes. Greatly improves probabilistic thinking.
This is a nice class for someone with a basic knowledge in probability to learn about stochastic processes. The analysis could be technical for a few times, yet all backgrounds are welcome!
This class is an interesting way of learning more about Markov chains. Since the class consists of 4 sets due every other week, the workload is somewhat heavy on the weeks that a set is due and light otherwise.
Really good class. The lecture notes are excellent - if you read them you can do all the HW.

The first 2 assignments are really easy, but takes some time to write up (lots of drawing Markov chains, some repetition, all of which is good for understanding the ideas). The 3rd assignment has some coding (office hours was good for resolving bugs), but the rest was again really chill. Assignment 4 is similarly easy as well, but takes some time to parse and generate ideas that lead to correct solutions because it's a bit more abstract.

Grading was also very chill - the TAs looked for the big picture idea in your solutions. if that was there, you would get all the credit (this probably has some variance since it depends on who the TAs are though).
Very interesting, clear and well-structured course.
