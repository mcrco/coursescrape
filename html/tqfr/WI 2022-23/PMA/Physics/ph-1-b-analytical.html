Expect to struggle a bit for that; but still very interesting overall. I will be taking physics 1c analytical to continue working with this professor, despite not being a physics option.

Ph1b analytical is a very interesting class and I would highly recommend it to anyone who is interested at all in Physics. So far, this has been one of my favorite classes at Caltech, both because of the content that was covered and how I exited the class feeling like I had really learned something. The structure of the class is very well-organized and the material is very fairly paced. If you have taken E&M before, you will find the pacing manageable. Each lecture highlights a couple different topics, but not so much that you feel exhausted and confused. Even if you took E&M in high school, having it retaught, and in a more complete sense, I found to be very useful and engaging.

As for the lectures, I would highly recommend going to them. Professor Hsieh is a phenomenal lecturer, perhaps one of if not the best I have had at Caltech so far. Professor Hsieh does a great job at explaining the physical intuition behind every step that they take, which is both interesting and important for developing a more complete understanding. In addition, they were always very approachable and would answer our questions with explanations that made the answer very clear. After walking out of the lectures, I genuinely feel like I learned something and wasn't completely lost, which was nice.

As for the textbooks, I would highly recommend reading them! The textbooks picked out for both the Special Relativity and E&M parts of the class are superb, and I especially found "Special Relativity" by Helliwell to provide very clear and logical derivations and explanations of the complex topics in relativity. Reading the text really does give you a more solid understanding and often helps a lot with solving the homework problems, so if you can, I would recommend keeping up with the readings.

As for the homework, it was not simple "plug and chug", and they definitely took a bit more time, especially once the class switches to E&M. The focus of the homework is really to help you develop a physical and problem-solving intuition, so they are often going to be challenging and not very straight forward, but I feel like they do help you grow. Simply put, they are challenging, require thought, and insightful, but not to the extreme where you are so stressed about even understanding the question and trying to get the set done. The quizzes and final, at least I felt, were also challenging.

All in all, if you are considering taking Ph1c analytical, I would recommend it, especially if you want to get a more concrete understanding of physics, regardless of your major.
