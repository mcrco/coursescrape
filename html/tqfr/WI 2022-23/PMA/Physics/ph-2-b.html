Pretty interesting and quite well taught course. I'm a MechE, so was skeptical about what I would get out of a quantum physics course. Turns out quantum physics is mostly just math? Having already taken Ma3 and ACM95 helped a lot with the statistics and sums.

Griffiths textbook is somewhat lacking in examples, so highly recommend going to office hours for questions about the specific problems on the homework.

As a senior, this was one of the least enjoyable classes I've taken in a long time.

The class is based around Griffiths' excellent textbook, and follows the first part of it almost to the letter. All of the material and all of the homework problems are taken from this book, which is the best thing I have to say about the class.

The lectures were usually rushed and routinely went over time, leaving most students confused. They also didn't cover everything needed for the sets, or brushed past the most useful material for completing the problems so fast it didn't register at all. Because of this, I strongly recommend reading the book over attending the lectures. It explains everything you need substantially more clearly and better paced.

In addition to homework sets, there are two "quizzes" (which are really midterms), each of which contributes 15% of the final grade, and a final exam worth 40%, so 70% of your grade is determined by exams. Beware that the quizzes also cover material before it appears on the sets. There is also no break from sets (or even shorter sets) on the weeks you have quizzes, and there are two sets for the week before the final exam. These weeks require a lot more than the nominal 9 hours.

Remember that this class is not for physics majors. No one taking it is primarily interested in pure physics, and everyone taking it has other classes which are more important for their major. There is no reason for it to be as painful as it is.
