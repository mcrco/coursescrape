Ma1b is a very nice course! I really like it! The teaching is very good! Although reading textbooks is helpful enough to do problem sets. The quizzes are sometimes toxic but it's manageable.
Something that I learned about this course was that if you scored a total of 85% or higher on your homework and quiz assignments, then you were excused from the final. The goal of this was for students with no prior experience in linear algebra to take the final, while the students with prior experience wouldn't need to take the final. This is to solidify your understanding of the course if you don't have as much experience with linear algebra as some other students do. It takes a while to truly understand the concepts in this class, so letting them sit, and then being tested on these ideas allows the professor to understand if you learned what was necessary from the course in the end. Don't feel bad if you cannot get above an 85% in this course. It is pass/fail and the course is to help you learn. :)
Problem sets are very manageable for this class. The textbook honestly makes concepts more confusing, and so do lectures. Your best bet for learning is going to rec, specifically Dylan's rec. Other than that the concepts are learnable on your own.
The class is really chill and the sets are not too difficult. You should hope Dylan is a TA because he is AMAZING! Also I would recommend trying to get above an 85% on the sets/quizzes so you don't have to take the final. But even if you do, the pass line was only a 60% so you'll definitely be fine.
Highly recommend aiming to get good scores on the sets and quizzes if possible. Use office hours, recitations, and lecture to get a full understanding of the course content and you will do well. The workload is manageable, just don't wait until the day before/ day of to do the set.
Three quizzes, weekly sets and a final (for some). There was a bit of time crunch for quizzes because if you chose a more inefficient method you didn't have enough time to complete all the questions within the time for full credit. Textbook is good to have because PS questions are taken directly from it. Could be useful to watch YouTube videos visually explaining the topics we cover analytically.
This class will be difficult if you've never taken linear algebra before. Good thing is, if you don't understand a concept, resources are available on YouTube to learn the material. Learn the material thoroughly before attempting the sets. Work with others in order to complete the problems and check your work, as some of the calculations can be a little tedious.
