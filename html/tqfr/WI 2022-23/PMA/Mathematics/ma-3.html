This class is badly organised under Professor Cvitanic. The class was in a flipped format this year, with videos of the previous year's lectures to watch at home and problem-solving practice in class. The lecture notes and slides are very helpful, and the homework is probably doable using only those. Problem-solving sessions could be a little slow because Prof. Cvitanic types out the solutions instead of using the chalkboard (and math takes a while to type), but he offered extra credit for volunteering to answer questions which was nice.
Honestly, not a big fan. The content was fine. I actually really like probability and statistics. The course kinda sucked though. More in an annoying way than a difficult way, but still. If you have to take this for your major, do it when other people you know are taking it. If you don't have to take it, unless it's in place of a much harder course, probably don't bother. The textbook and posted lecture slides are pretty useful though, I will say that.

This course relies on your exam performance and on correctness. If all of your logical steps are correct and recognized to be correct by the TA, you will still get 4/5 of the points marked off. Do not screw up on the midterm or final.
The grading in this course (at least this year) was unfair and unnecessarily harsh, with huge amounts of points being taken off for relatively minor mistakes. The final grading also seemed to be extremely skewed, as a grade over 91% was curved to a B+.
The backwards curve wasn't very nice. Other than that it was a fine class.
homeworks are very doable (consult the notes, they're great). the midterm was ok. wasn't a big fan of the grading on the final, but it was ok too.
Chances are, this course is a major requirement for you, so there isn't much to say in terms of recommending whether or not you take this course. However, this is a pretty good introduction to probability and statistics, and it lays a solid foundation for more advanced statistics classes. There are 8 problem sets, the lowest score of which will be dropped - these are pretty manageable, though the problem sets post-midterm tend to be more tedious and thus take longer. The midterm and final are pretty difficult, and do not correlate perfectly with the homework (especially the final). The lecture slides and lecture notes for this class are amazing - I would strongly recommend utilizing them while working on assignments, as the questions tend to follow closely from these resources. Overall, this is a roughly properly-united class that you'll have to do work for each week, though you hopefully won't be overwhelmed on any particular week.

I recommend first couple weeks to go to all recs to check them out and pick out which TAs you like the most.
