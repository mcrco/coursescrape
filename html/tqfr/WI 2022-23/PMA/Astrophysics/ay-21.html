Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? This class is honestly not too bad so long as you keep a consistent study schedule as each lecture happens. There are only four problem sets and both the midterm and final are closed book. It's a good deal of equation/slide hunting so it can be a bit of a slog if you don't keep up.
Overall good class, office hours are very helpful in finishing the sets.
Lectures are definitely fast paced as it is mostly reading from/explaining slides, but the fact that they are all recorded is very useful. I ended up skipping lectures because I would try to go in person but would always need to rewatch later at my own pace, so I stopped attending.

You cannot always answer questions on the sets with things from lecture. A lot of it is figuring out how to use general equations or things discussed in Ay 20. The lecture material is more useful on the exams, which are more qualitative.

That being said, exams are closed note which is never fun. As long as you have a good TA that makes sure you are prepared to study for those, it is alright. If the TA does not do that, it will be a long exam.
This was a fun class, the material was interesting and we only had 4 problem sets. However, the midterm and final were both closed notes. This made me sad as I cannot remember formulas. :(
Fun class, interesting content. Lectures were good, but very fast paced and often left us guessing as to what material was important. Only had one set every 2 weeks so that was nice, and they weren't especially hard. The exams were closed book and close notes which was very sad.
Pretty decent course. I took this as a substitute for Ay1 for my menu requirement as I already knew I enjoy cosmology/LSS more than general astronomy. The TA was amazing, the HWs were informative and at a good difficulty. My only qualms are that the exams focus a surprising amount on memorization facts that can't be reproduced (but can be easily Googled) and the lectures themselves.

The course is a mile-wide, inch-deep sort-of course, and this makes it extremely difficult to tell what is or isn't important in the course. That, and the style of lectures (numerous, data-heavy graphs) make this FAR more difficult than it should be. I personally think this course could really benefit from a more heuristic style of teaching and going more into depth (and understanding) of some core concepts.

The sets however make up for this by being straight forward enough to try (and understand their importance), as well as challenging and interesting enough. However the later half of the course (galaxy formation/LSS) feels more guestimating than it perhaps should be. The cosmology half however is nearly perfect.
This is an awesome core course for astro majors and anyone interested in cosmology/galaxies. The content is really interesting and Prof. Djorgovski is a very passionate lecturer. His lecture slides can be really dense at times and makes it hard to review for problem sets and exams, but there are only 4 problem sets for the whole term (due about every other week) which allow for more time to focus on the concepts being covered in lecture. Like Ay 20, the exams are closed book so there is a lot of memorization needed when you're studying, but otherwise the exams closely reflect the problem sets and most students are able to succeed and do very well in this class. Please take it if you are considering it!
