What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

Really great course and the homework is amazing and definitely geared towards bettering your understanding. Piazza and online support are very helpful, and the clas structure with regard to grading, exams, and extensions makes for a stress-free course. Really enjoyed it. Would highly recommend looking into representation theory, complex analysis, and Lie theory prior to taking the course to have a better mathematical intuition.
tl;dr: I enjoyed this class, and you probably will too.

Prof. Porter's Ph 125a is a great class and I highly recommend it to anyone who studies anything related to QM.

The class material is certainly hard and it takes time to understand it (but it's QM - so this is kinda expected), but it's also incredibly interesting. The problem sets for this class have unique questions that are fun to think about and work through, and that are also helpful to understand the topics covered in lecture. Additionally, the TAs and Prof. Porter help work through the problem sets during office hours (of which there are many: 5 each week; 2 TA office hours and 3 with Prof. Porter) - which is **incredibly** helpful not just to complete the problems but also to understand the science occurring.

Although Ph 12b is the official prerequisite for this course, I think that Ch 21a and Ch 21b also do a good job of preparing chemistry majors. It's probably a good idea to review Griffiths before Ph 125a, and also probably a good idea to have a collaboration group to work and discuss with.

I would definitely recommend this class to chemistry majors interested in p-chem and/or inorganic chemistry, since a lot of concepts discussed are chemically relevant (ex. angular momentum, selection rules, the group theory involved in both, etc.). It's also really interesting to experience QM from a different perspective than chemistry (and to see how ideas in both fields overlap).

The official textbook for this class is Shankar, but I think that Sakurai, Gottfried, Prof. Porter's notes, and other online resources (ex. Littlejohn's QM notes) are more useful than Shankar.

I found that attending lecture for this class is *very* helpful since I think that a lot of this material needs to be seen (at least) twice and questioned (at least) once to understand it. If you go to lecture, you've at least seen the material for the first time, and Prof. Porter helps answer any questions anyone has.
All assignments were problem sets which made the class a lot easier to handle than in past years. Midterm is just no collaboration problem set but you can still ask questions in office hours. Final problem set is optional.
