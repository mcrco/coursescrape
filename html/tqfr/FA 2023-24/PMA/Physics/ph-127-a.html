Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

The course will involve quite some thinking and working through problems, but everything is rewarding and very useful especially if you are interested in physics research.
If you are interested in statistical physics or condensed matter physics, this course is a must-take. It is very well organized, and each assignment serves a purpose. Overall a very good class.
The course is fine. If you are interested in the material, it may be enjoyable. I was not. But it was similar difficulty to other courses in the core and the Professor is a good lecturer. Homeworks/final are reasonable and on par with Ph125, Ph106 etc.
I enjoyed this class more than any other class this term (junior physics major so comparing to 125 and 106). This class is hard but very worth it if you're interested in the subject matter.
Well taught course, Motrunich's lecture notes are genuinely beautiful. The material is very esoterically stat mech, with only a couple different systems actually discussed throughout the quarter, so you will not find much applicability if you aren't interested in second order magnetic phase transitions. However, the mathematical tools introduced are pretty fundamental to the field and good to learn if you are going to continue in physics.
