What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

I am really bad at physics but I still managed to get through this course. It's important to go to recitations since the TAs focus on solving problems related to the homework. Figure out which TAs are the best for you and attend their rec sessions. Some of the homeworks were super long in terms of doing pages and pages of math by hand, but the rec TAs will give you a roadmap to follow for solving the long problems. Also the midterm and final have lots of hints so that you can check if your answers are correct and feel good about yourself.
This is a great course. It is manageable and interesting. The class is organized and there are plenty of resources/help if you need it.
This class was extremely easy and not much work at all. The TAs essentially walked through most of the problem set each week, and both midterms (the final was not cumulative) were reasonable. Going to lecture was not necessary at all, I stopped going after two weeks since the lecture notes were posted on Canvas.

That being said, this class felt much more like a math class than a physics class. Rather than learning about the physical intuition behind things, we mostly just focused on how to solve specific problems. The final didn't require any physics knowledge at all if you had a good understanding of differential equations and Fourier series. The algebra on the sets felt unnecessarily tedious at times, because it had nothing to do with helping us understand the physics more. I guess that's what you get in a physics class for non-physics majors, but nonetheless it was still a little disappointing.
Sergi is a great professor. He is very understanding and kind to his students, and his course policies very much reflect that. This is a good class.

Great class, especially if you have a lot of other things on your plate. Sergi is very passionate about teaching and tries to make concepts relevant to each student's major. He is also quick to respond to questions on Piazza and is generally very accommodating.

We covered waves with regards to circuits, simple harmonic motion/potential energy wells, coupled springs, and standing/transverse waves. We ended with a brief introduction to quantum mechanics. There was a section in the middle where we covered diffraction and there were no lecture slides for those, so it was quite important to go to class.

Some of the problem sets can have pretty thorny algebra, which recitations help walk you through. Also, the final and midterm are non-collab, but there are hints within the questions themselves that suggest what manipulations to make and guide you to the expected result.
