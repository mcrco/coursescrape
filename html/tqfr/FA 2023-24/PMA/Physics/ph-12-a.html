Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

Don't take this class unless it is a major requirement. Class was an unreasonable amount of work, and it somehow made me like waves even less. The assignments are just immensely long, and there are some parts that you would never figure out unless you sought help.
Tough but interesting
This course is tough, and problem sets are time-consuming and very difficult. Assignments were due at noon on Tuesdays, and the majority of Monday nights I was up working on Ph12a with my peers until 2 AM or later, despite having started assignments the same day they were released. I'm sure I spent more time on this course than my other courses combined. Supposedly, having exposure to this more advanced version of waves will help with upper-level Ph courses, I certainly hope so, however I can't comment on that just yet. :)
Great class overall, interesting lectures and sets, but be prepared for 10-12 hour problem sets and a challenging midterm and final. Work with other people and do not work on the sets at the last minute otherwise it will be very overwhelming. Go to office hours and recitations, they are very helpful. I would only take this course if you are majoring in physics/astrophysics or are extremely bright because of the amount of time commitment the class is.
You probably will only be taking this class if you have to for your major, so there's not much I can say on whether or not to take it :). However, just know that this class moves very fast and assumes some sort of either fast comprehension or the ability to go through math really fast in lecture (without having all the steps written out) in order to follow. Also, it helps if you have more time to dedicate to this class, it's hard to balance understanding it very well when you take multiple other classes. Find people to work with!
It's a course that is quite challenging and requires a good amount of time commitment. Going to OH and recitations is extremely helpful, but only if you've already started the homework. This is a class where it pays to try and stay on track, because it is extremely fast-paced, and although the lecture notes are helpful, they are extremely long, so it is good to try and stay on top of things.
For those who are taking this course in the future, understand that although it isn't easy, it is worth it. This class has a high level of rigor (and a pretty big workload) but you will leave knowing that you have truly learned something. Definitely worth it and Zmuidzinas is an amazing professor.
I took this class as chem major hoping to have a better understanding of waves than Ph 2a. Unfortunately this class conflicted with one of my other classes so I was unable to attend lectures. For any one in a similar situation (or any chem majors considering taking this over Ph 2a), I would HIGHLY recommend auditing this class and taking Ph 2a. Only the first couple weeks is relevant to chemistry. The psets are unnecessarily long and (for me at least) did not contribute much to a physical understanding of the concepts. Pset grading also took an extremely long time, and most sets before the midterm were not grading until right before the midterm.
Highly recommend taking it, especially if you have to take Ph 2a anyways! I learned a lot and heavily enjoyed this class. It's very well taught.
Really great class, lots of interesting physics! Some problem sets are a bit rougher than others but just keep working through them as they help you solidify your understanding of the material. Thursday lectures usually had demos which were also fun.
With the exception of last three weeks, the content was not particularly difficult to grasp. Unlike last year, the midterm was also completely reasonable, both in difficulty and in time constraints. I really enjoyed the course, as Professor Zmuidzinas was a fantastic lecturer and the waves were really interesting. For anyone with a choice between Ph 12a and Ph 2a, I would say that there is no reason to be afraid of taking Ph 12a!
Professor Zmuidzinas rewrote the course this year (Fall 2023). Compared to previous years, I felt that the material better prepared us for Ph12b Quantum Mechanics. His notes were spectacular and very detailed. The notes for this class were so good, I will probably end up re-reading them during break for more information that I might not have been able to look over during term.
That being said, my advice for this course is to always look over the course notes before sincerely attempting the sets. Often you will find that the course notes help a lot with understanding the course material. Anything you can't get from the course notes and then attempting the sets, you can then always go to Recitation/OH to get more help with strategies and tricks to figure out the problems. The TAs this year (and I am assuming next year) were very good.
