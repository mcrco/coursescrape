A nice introduction to group theory
This is a great class, especially if you are new to proof-based mathematics. For the more experienced student, it can move a bit slow at times, so keep this in mind.
Even if you are not a Math major (I am a Computer Science major, but I am very interested in group theory), this is a must-take course. Professor Gherman makes group theory very accessible and after this class, I think I will remember so much of the content.

The homeworks were very reflective of what was taught in class. While some were challenging, they were not impossible to solve. Furthermore, Professor Gherman ensures that we remember each of the concepts by offering problem-solving worksheets (that extend our knowledge of the content covered) for completion points (yet we still take them quite seriously) and forum posts that allow us to answer questions about group theory based on the content taught in class that week (also for completion points, yet we make an effort to ensure we are learning the content). Gherman's office hours are amazing; he ensures we understand all of the homework content and even when we need to understand how one step of the proof leads to another, he patiently explains it to all of us.

Gherman is so prompt to mails regarding the class content, such as doubts on a proof or clarifications on a course assignment.

The midterm was quite lengthy and did require some thought, but was overall a fun exam! There is no final exam for this course, but rather a final project in which we pick a group of our choice and examine its significance, properties, proofs, and research.

I think I gained so much from this course, and I would definitely recommend this course to anybody else.
