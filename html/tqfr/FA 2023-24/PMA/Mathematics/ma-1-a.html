This course was excellent. I really enjoyed Math 1a and this course made me more interested in math in general (I am an applied physics major). For example, Piccard's Theorem or Weierstrass substitution is something I had never heard about before. Additionally, the level of rigor was nice (supremums, bolzano's theorem, partitions for integration, etc.). I would highly recommend doing Math 0 beforehand and also reading a bit of real analysis (Understanding Analysis by Abbott is good). Professor Conlon is very knowledgeable and the lectures are clear (although sometimes too fast or slow). Doing the psets was also fun as the problems were the right difficulty most of the time.
The course restructuring was done pretty well imo; the content and psets were challenging and interesting but not too firehose, and actually got easier later into the year (went from mostly proofs to a mix of computation and proofs, maybe cuz Conlon took pity on us lmao..the final was also significantly easier than the midterm). Do go to lecture though, since the proofs/tricks he does in class are often used in the homework. There's one pset per week with 5 questions, which are a pretty good mix of hard and easier/medium questions. I hadn't done a lot of proof stuff in high school (only very very basic ones), so this class actually did teach me a lot about analysis/doing proofs in general and just thinking about them in a mathier way. Math 0 was aslo pretty helpful, at least in the first week (the first lecture is pretty much a crash course on math 0). Conlon does jump over some steps during lecture, but if you ask him he's very good at breaking it down. Also do go to office hours, since sometimes, for me at least, I didn't know where to even start on certain questions, and OH pretty much told you how to start and what tricks to use.
