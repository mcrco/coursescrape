This course is a pretty well-balanced course; I had some experience in coding (from AP CS back in high school), which definitely made homework (called Mini Projects where you add code to provided files to build something larger, like a mini game) easier, but El and the TAs are extremely supportive, from providing extra office hours to just working through things with you. Homework took maybe 3 hours per week, and each Mini Project was pretty consistent in length. The lectures though were not very helpful, since they were just kinda unstructured (like sometimes the instructor would go on tangents for most of the 1-hour lecture time and would barely go over the material for the week). Self-studying and going through the provided readings was way more helpful for me.

The content of the course itself though is very intersting and relevant to people of all options. A new unit for us was matplotlib/data visualization, which was cool! Many mini projects are also oriented around some kind of research topic, like visualizing lattices, since El did a lot of past research in bio/genetic programming. If you put in the work and study/do the readings/stay on top of things, this course is very managable, though I can definitely see it being more difficult for those with no past coding experience (given the lectures).
