This class makes theoretical machine very accessible to all. All the lectures were extremely interesting. Furthermore, Professor Yaser introduces New Takes that allow us to see the applications of machine learning and current advances in the field.

Sets take approximately on average 5 to 7 hours per week. There is substantial coding in each assignment, but a significant amount of code in the earlier sets gets reused in the later sets. This does mean you should ensure your earlier code works and runs in a short amount of time. Lecture slides are also immensely helpful in solving the homework questions (which are multiple choice, but do ensure you explain your reasoning for each question)

Office hours are quite helpful for checking derivations to the problems on the set.

It also helps that the final occurs before the designated time for most finals. It helps alleviate the stress during that time of year.
