Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

If you are considering Electrical engineering as a potential major, I would highly recommend the class. It teaches you a lot about different motors and motor control as well as conversion between electrical and mechanical energy and vice versa. If you are considering Mechanical engineering it can be pretty helpful to have a good understanding of the electronics behind the motors; however, this class leans a lot more towards the electrical side than the mechanical side. There is no coding at all and it is purely circuit design for control. If you are not considering electrical engineering or mechanical engineering as a major it will likely not be very relevant and is a lot more work than 6 hrs per week. It is probably more like 10-16hrs per week depending on what the labs are that week. If you are not at all interested in mechatronics it is probably more work than its worth, but if you are slightly interested it is a pretty cool class, and if you don't have much previous background you will learn a lot. Make sure you go to the TA office hours because they are very helpful.
this is a great course! it is unfortunately closer to a 15 unit course than a 6 unit one. (i'm not exaggerating, i calculated this.) but if you're really passionate about electrical or mechanical engineering, you can definitely get a lot out of it; i don't regret taking it at all. just go in knowing the workload is more than two times the advertised amount
If you want to learn a lot, you will learn a lot. This class is pretty fun but it was ALOT of work. More than 12 hours a week. Two labs a week but the content is very interesting. I am CS major with no EE experience but now I feel confident I can have EE side projects and understand what I am doing.
"Underunited" very well but obviously describes this course. If you have prior experience in EE, then you might be able to get away with spending 8-9 hours in the lab per week. If not, expect to be spending 10-15. With all this said, though, Professor George is a very intelligent and kind person; his course will absolutely ensure that you walk away with an intuitive understanding of various mechatronic systems. Also, as long as you submit all the lab reports and do acceptably well on the final project, you will likely pass. If you want a challenge and endeavor an introduction to EE, I very much recommend this course.
This is severely under united. Strongly recommend taking an intro course to electronics/getting acquainted with circuitry before this because it will make it a lot easier. A lot of the times you won't understand why or why not your lab worked. Make sure to clear doubts about this early on in office hours with TAs or Glen. Make sure you turn in all of the labs and be prepared to spend a significant amount of time in the lab.
