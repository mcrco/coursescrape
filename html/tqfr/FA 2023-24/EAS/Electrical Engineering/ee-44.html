Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

You are most likely a s'more EE taking this course and there's lots of stories that this class can push people too hard or to new limits. I do think that is true from my experience in the class. I personally found that 44 was my class. It really inspired me to take myself seriously and push myself to achieve new goals. Even if the exam scores did not turn out great, I know I took away a lot from the class. I always felt incredibly accomplished from finishing the Problem Set Packets, and I had a greater appreciation for the content. I also thoroughly enjoyed talking with Haj after lecture on occasion and talking with the TAs about the content. This class confirmed that I was in the right major and that it was a good fit for me. I found it overall really enjoyable and I was always interested in devoting the hours to understand the content and work on the set. There is definitely nothing else that I would rather do than EE after taking this class. It also made other EE classes more enjoyable, and it provides a really solid understanding of core concepts in major. I have taken other requirements and this class definitely helps with understanding the subjects. I thoroughly enjoyed this class, and I hope other future EEs share the sentiment.
Be prepared to have to learn a lot of things on your own, without having them explained by the instructor, because you will be tested on them regardless.
The course picks up rapidly, make sure to start early on the sets and preparations for exams. Exams are tough, but the course is taught extremely well.
Start your sets early and don't overload if you can while taking 44. Some sets can take a lot more time than you expect. Please review your math skills and especially algebra - that's really the heart of the math in this class. Professor Hajimiri is a great lecturer, probably one of the best at Caltech, and he loves to engage with his students. Go to class (there are pop quizzes), go to OH, and be ready for the hardest 10 weeks of your life. There is so much material in this class and after it's done, you'll hopefully have learned a lot.
Very good class. Professor Hajimiri really makes lectures worth every minute of your time. Problem sets teach you to analyze circuits such as buck-boost converters that are used practically. And office hours are immensely useful for getting tips for the sets without dropping the answers in your lap.

This is known as a difficult class, most likely for the following reasons:

- The sets are long. Many classes have sets with 5-10 questions (including subparts). A typical 44 set has around 25 parts. Additionally, each set has a chapter of required reading, which you often can't skip because Professor Hajimiri doesn't cover the corresponding material at all/until after the set is due.

- There's a lot of content. Enough said. Most of it is not too difficult conceptually, but it can take time for things to click enough to solve problems with them. On the bright side, the PDF version of the textbook was finally licensed to our class this year: https://chic.caltech.edu/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Artice-Davis-Linear-Circuit-Analysis-s4.pdf

- Math. So many problems just end in gnarly algebra. Almost every circuit analysis technique we learned relied heavily on algebra: matrices for nodal/mesh analysis, integrals for convolution, complex numbers for phasors, partial fraction expansion for operators/Laplace, etc…
Professor Hajimiri noted that our class was particularly weak in math, and subsequently performed significantly worse on the math skills quiz and midterm than previous classes. That shows that if you're not fluent in algebra in this class, you will struggle a lot.

- The exams are as long as and harder than the sets, but you only have 2-4 hours to do them, with only a single equation sheet to guide you. Plus, the exams often try to "teach you something new".

- "Double set" weeks: Many classes will not assign a set during midterm/finals week. This one does! What's more, the midterms/finals sets are due earlier than usual to "give us time to study" for the exams. Thus, you have to do 2 sets in a little over a week. This takes some pretty extreme time management to balance with all your other classes.

Even though the class was very rough, I found it quite reasonable (perhaps apart from double set week during midterms). Expectations were laid out clearly from the start, the workload was even, and the time commitment was not outlandish. I feel like I still have so much to learn, but I definitely got a lot out of the class.
