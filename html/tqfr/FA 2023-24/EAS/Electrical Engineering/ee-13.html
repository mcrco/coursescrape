Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

This class only really has three assignments: the schematic, the PCB design, and the soldering. They have deadlines, but they're very spaced out and... known to be a bit loose. If you're the sort of person who puts things away then you might find yourself struggling at the end of the course. If you're excited to learn, design, and build a PCB over the span of the whole term, than this class is great for you.
This course is really good if you're interested in making physical electronics but you don't know where to start.
It can be a bit stressful. Be super good about checking your email and getting back to the instructor for fixes on your PCB, because it takes about a week to arrive after ordering and it's extremely stressful to try to get your project done as you approach the end of term. Soldering is therapeutic IMO but ymmv.
I would recommend it, it feels awesome to hold a working project after hours of work.
Although this class is theoretically 3 units, it's more like 9 units every 3 weeks unless you have good time management skills so plan accordingly.
This was a great class and I am happy I took it, however be aware that the objective is NOT how the circuit works/how to design a circuit but instead how to take an already designed circuit (you chose from 3 options) and create an actual physical PCB out of it
The workload is very heavily grouped towards the three different assignments and it's definetly necessary to start early as you have to get individually checked off in office hours and the longer you wait the more people will be in OH so it can easily take 1-2 hours just to get checked off
Only 4 lectures throughout term which aren't super necessary but are interesting and provide a lot more details. The "how to" of the assignments are taught by TAs in tutorials and how helpful that is definitely depends on the TA but you absolutely need to attend those
For the first 2 weeks you won't do any work, then you will create the schematic which will take a couple of hours. Then for a week or 2 you won't do any work again, and then you'll work on your PCB wiring. Then you won't do work for a couple of weeks again and then you'll have to do all the soldering in the last week. The class isn't hard at all and it doesn't require any serious thinking. It's just really tedious. In addition to this, the last week will be super annoying because there will be so many students trying to solder at the same time. Just make sure to start early!
Don't procrastinate on the schematic or the PCB layout. This class is fun although I was sleep-deprived while working on the homework XD
