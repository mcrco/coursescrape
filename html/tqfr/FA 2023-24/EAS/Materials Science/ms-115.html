Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

Scott is a terrific prof and this was probably my favourite course so far at Caltech. He oozes passion about the material, has a great sense of humor, and explains hard-to-understand topics terrifically. I would take this class again just to listen to his stories about his various escapades working at NASA, which he frequently incorporates into the material and provides a real-world basis for abstract concepts I wouldn't have normally cared about (I have been rambling like a lunatic to all of my friends about steelmaking for a month now). He can be a bit scatterbrained at times (the final came out 2 days before the due date, can go on tangents sometimes and not cover some material, no real syllabus, really bad wording on a few things, etc.) but is incredibly nice and understanding. Overall, the class is definitely on the easier side as well. I didn't need office hours on sets and could finish most of them in just a few hours. The midterm and final were both very relaxed. There was a fun project in the middle of the course about how everyday items are manufactured from raw materials that was very laid-back as well. I would recommend this class to anyone with a remote interest in materials science and hope Scott decides to teach it again/more classes in the future as well!
Interesting speeches, demonstration experiments that draw attention, responsible and friendly teaching assistants, and students who actively participate in discussions. What course is better than this?
As of now, the class is a bit scattered. However, Prof. Roberts is really passionate about the material and obviously has his heart in the right place. It should hopefully be a really great course in future terms.

The class itself is a combination of some solid state chemistry, some crystallography, some thermodynamics, and some kinetics. Not really in depth at any of these points, but good breadth. Good for anyone who just wants to see if materials science is the right field for them.
