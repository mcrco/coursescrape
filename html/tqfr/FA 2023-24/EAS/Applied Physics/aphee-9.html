Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

Take this class and don't look back. If you are at all interested in this course (look at the REGIS description, it matches closely), you will not be disappointed. The content is technical, but presented in a very understandable manner. If you don't understand something, ask a question mid lecture and Scherer will happily go into more detail. Aside from lectures, the weekly labs are always easy and engaging but most importantly, will give you experiences that you will likely never have aside from in hyper-specific research. Finally, Scherer is incredibly genuine, extremely knowledgeable, and quite fun to be around. Even though attendance is not taken, if you decide to skip lectures, you will just be missing out. Course work is also easy, there was only 1 short homework, then 7 three page lab reports. No quizzes/tests other than multiple choice, unlimited time, open internet final (subject to change).

TL;DR: This is probably the most chill frosh lab that is super interesting with a world-class professor, if you think you might like it, take it.
This course is a bit of a mess overall. Scherer is an incredibly nice guy and a decent lecturer, but goes on tangents often and ends up leaving most of the class confused. I feel like the ta's were incredibly hit or miss as well. Some were unable to answer basic questions about the material whereas others were very knowledgeable. The canvas is very poorly maintained and contains lots of older information and some content that's not even in the course anymore. I know at least two people who have done the wrong assignment because the actual one was hidden in emailed PowerPoint slides rather than the posted questions under the actual submission in Canvas. The grading seems random and arbitrary, no assignments were graded until the last week of the term and there is no feedback on any of them, which one can imagine is incredibly frustrating, especially on grades.
I really enjoyed Aph 9. As an applied physics major, I found this course to be an interesting introduction to solid state devices (pn junctions, depletion width, vacuum pumps, etc). I would highly recommend this course as an introductory lab. Professor Scherer is very chill and has a lot of experience. The labs in this course are great, but note that the combined load of the lectures, labs, and lab reports (fairly easy) makes this course slightly under-unit-ed (require more time than 6 units (hours)). The only improvement I would make to the course is to have a summary at the end of each lecture because there is a lot of information covered.
This class is definitely a fun and interesting class and will satisfy the intro lab requirement you need for the core. It is a really chill class....the time requirement each week was about 3 hours for the weekly lab, 2ish for the lab reports, and 2 if you showed up to lecture (which is worth going just not many people did). Also the labs are pretty interesting and very light compared to other lab classes like Chem 3x when it comes to workload. You also do learn quite a bit from them and the soldering lab was my favorite and made me want to learn soldering more.

However, there are a few things about this class that I hope they fix in the future. One is the grading of the homework/lab reports. This is probably the worst and most obscure class when it comes to this aspect that I have taken here at Caltech. I am writing this one day after classes have ended and still haven't gotten back about half of the assignments including the first homework....which I think should not be the case. Luckily, like most people, I took this class P/F so it doesn't matter too much BUT if you decide to take this class on grades expect to have no clue what your grade is going to be until grades come out (probably will be B-A range but you won't know). So I would highly recommend taking on P/F....it will save yourself so much time and stress if you worry about your GPA. Also, the final was VERY hard and time consuming when compared to other assignments.....it was around 40 questions and a few questions had 6-7 true and false in just one question. Again, taking P/F so didn't worry too much about it and you can use anything online....but I had a buddy taking it on grades and he spent ALOT of time completing it because he probably had to verify and check about 100 questions. Plus, I still have no idea how it is going to be weighted or curved or if the 7 part questions are all or nothing......so just take it P/F if you don't want to worry about it too much.
