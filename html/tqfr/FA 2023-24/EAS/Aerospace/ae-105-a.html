Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

Very good course! Taught by people who actually do this stuff for JPL. I would recommend it to physics majors, engineers etc. The workload is not bad and the material is fun to engage with.
Honestly, I loved this class! Probably, this class was one of the most interesting ones I have ever taken in my life. I am particularly interested in astrodynamics and found the content trully exciting. The HWs are a crucial component of the learning process. Be prepared to spend several hours a week on the psets (something from 5h to 12h, depending on the HW). However, once you finish them, it is really rewarding to see the final result.
Overall, I would recommend this course to anyone interested in astrodynamics / space engineering / applied math.
I would not recommend this course -- it covers too many topics in too little time (not all of which are even related to astrodynamics), so there is not enough detail given on any particular topic to make it useful. The lectures are difficult to follow, and it is hard to see how one topic relates to the next. The lectures are only tangentially connected to the homeworks, and the lecture schedule and homework deadlines slipped multiple times such that 2 homework assignments (making up 40% of the course grade) were due during finals week.
Do not take this course unless you are highly interested in modeling space trajectories. This class is almost entirely coding in Matlab using SPICE. If you are not already competent in Matlab, then you should not take this class. The lectures are not super helpful in doing the assignments. There is only one TA whose office hours fill up too fast to get any truly personal assistance. If you do take this class start your assignments very early and expect them to take 9+ hours.
