I think this is a great course introduction to the thermal sciences.
ME 11a is a really fun and interesting course. As someone who is interested in thermo/fluids, I think that ME 11a has some really interesting content.

The course covers the basics of thermo, starting from an introduction to state variables, then goes into the first law, the second law/entropy, and then applications. All in all, there is not a ton of new content, and you might already be familiar with most of the general thermo knowledge from Ch1b. However, it does get introduced in a pretty unique way and with formulas/models that you might not have seen before. Conceptually, this class can kind of confuse you at times. Thermodynamics is not usually something (at least for me, anyways) that can be presented once and you get it immediately. It might take time to truly internalize what you are learning. I recommend that you dedicate some time outside of class to go back through the lecture slides/read the corresponding sections in the book. Making sure that you have a solid conceptual understanding of the content is key, as it all builds off of each other.

As for homeworks, they were very manageable. They do not take forever or have many computations, so it is pretty reasonable. That being said, I would highly recommend that you start the sets for this class as soon as you can. It can be easy to just go to office hours and figure out how to do the problems on the set, but if your goal is to truly internalize the material and develop both the intuition and the problem-solving skills of thermodynamics, starting early and giving yourself enough time to think through the problems and figure out as much of them as you can alone is key and will definitely help you out. Still, though, go to office hours to check your assumptions and make sure you didn't incorrectly make any that you shouldn't have.

As for Professor Blanquart, he has been one of my best professors here at Caltech. He really is just the right person to be teaching thermodynamics. He has very illustrative lecture notes, and his teaching style during lecture is very engaging and effective. He makes a great effort to answer any questions you might have and really wants to see you learning. His office hours are also super helpful. Even outside of being a great professor, he really cares about students, and is very receptive to any feedback or concerns.

All in all, ME 11a is a cool class, can be conceptually difficult, but Professor Blanquart and the TAs make it very reasonable and enjoyable.
