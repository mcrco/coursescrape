I will warn you ahead of time that this course has a heavy workload, but it helps teach you the fundamentals you need to know as an engineer so it is worth it. Just make sure to put an effort into this course and you should be fine.

However, there are a lot of very useful resources provided and the lectures are extremely helpful. It was by far my favorite class of the term but be prepared to live in office hours and spend many hours on the sets. Me 12 is a great class. It is part of MechE core for good reason, and it will definitely become one of your most important classes this term. It is super fun conceptually but is also super involved.

The course starts with an introduction to forces and moments and how to calculate them, and then goes onto how to discuss how to do equilibrium problems with them. From there, you will do truss structures and truss analysis before the midterm. Then after the midterm, you pivot (no pun intended lol) into bending and shear stresses in beam analysis, before you then expand this to axial and shear stress and strain, torsional moments, and then thermal expansion/contractions and other sorts of misfits and prestrain problems. The course then concludes with a discussion of fluid statics. All in all, pretty fun material!

Professor Mello is a really great professor and does a fantastic job at teaching this course. They really do care about teaching this class since it is so fundamental to you becoming an engineer, and so they provide tons of resources to help you learn the material and do well on the sets. Going to all lectures is my first recommendation. Just being there and listening, at least I have found, keeps you up to date with whatever material the class is currently covering, and will help you fully understand it conceptually. I also recommend you go to all of the problem-solving recitations. Professor Mello specifically designs and dedicates these sessions to teaching you what you need to know in order to do well on the current set. Often, he will go through similar problems. Going to them consistently helps you really know where to start with the problems you end up doing on the set instead of just getting stuck. All in all, the lectures, recitations, and other posted notes on Canvas are all there to help you out in this class.

One thing to note, though, is that the sets do take quite a bit. You definitely want to start them at least 2 days before the deadline if you can, these really cannot be left until the last minute. They require lots of computations and there are tons of places where your math can go wrong. So, always start as early as you can, and double check your work. Cannot stress enough that you must start them as soon as you can, even if it means doing little bits and pieces of problems during short times throughout your day.

All in all, ME12a is a great class, Professor Mello is a phenomenal lecturer and professor, but the class can be involved so definitely prioritize it to get the most out of it.
