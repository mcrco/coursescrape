Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

This is a great introductory robotics class and one of the best classes I've taken at Caltech so far. Gunter's lectures are great, and they're also recorded. The HW assignments are reflective of the topics we cover in class, and although sometimes tedious, are fairly doable without the help of the TA. Professor Gunter overall is a great teacher and is very lenient with deadlines if you ask appropriately.
ME133a is a unique combination of mathematical analysis, mechanical engineering, and robotics. You apply alot of linear algebra to make robots do cool things and its lots of fun! That being said, the sets are quite challenging but I personally thought it was worth it.
Great course! I loved the content and the mini project at the end. I had so much fun doing the project with my friends and I feel like I learned a lot in this class. If the course was more organized (meaning the sets were out on-time), I feel like I would have enjoyed it more. But overall, I really really appreciate Gunter's time and effort being put into this class. He really really cares about the students -- super accessible and happy to meet with students to answer questions even on weekends.

This is a really cool robotics course! Günter is an amazing prof who puts in so much time outside of lecture to make sure everyone succeeds. There are sets for the first 7 weeks and two quizzes (which are a bit tricky but were curved) and the last 3 weeks is a final project. I really enjoyed the material of the course as I'm really interested in robotics but I don't have much academic experience with robotics at all. The latter part of this course gets a bit coding heavy but I also know some MechEs who did very well as well :) Also it was nice to take ME/CS/EE 129 in the spring before taking this class but not everyone did.
