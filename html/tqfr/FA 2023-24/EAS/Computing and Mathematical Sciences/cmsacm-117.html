Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

go to bootcamp, otherwise just enjoy
I found it to be a very good class that went into a lot of detail into the fundamentals of probability and derived a ton of inequalities in a way that I found makes it much clearer to see when they are tight bounds. Understanding the derivation and proof of Hoeffding's inequality makes it immediately clear when it gives you a fairly tight bound and when it's throwing away a ton of information to apply it, for example.

Professor Tropp is an excellent lecturer and I found his analogies and examples to be useful and well thought out, as well as engaging and often entertaining.

The homework in this class is fairly long, but seemed to correspond fairly well to the unit requirement of the class and was broadly interesting (though I did find some of it to be rather straight forward on an intuitive level and tedious in its execution). Having some background in measure theory is not necessary (especially since next year, Professor Tropp is planning to add back in a few lectures dedicated to measure theory, whereas this year we had none) but I found it to be very helpful in the earlier portions of the class.

Overall, I found it to be an excellent class and I loved the way it, among other things, unified discrete and continuous probability without informal appellations to "limits."
A fun yet challenging course
This course will take a lot of time, so make sure you take it with people you know, but it is very well taught and quite a bit of fun!
An analysis background is helpful. The hardest topics of the class are right at the end, so don't get screwed by end of quarter fatigue.
Joel is a very good lecturer. He follows his own lecture notes that he shares, but it is very helpful to physically attend the lecture.
He responds to the questions on the Piazza quite frequently and it is very helpful.
In the comments from the previous years, many people are saying the amount of homework is a lot, but I think Joel reduced the amount this year.
This is a rigorous yet very manageable course.
