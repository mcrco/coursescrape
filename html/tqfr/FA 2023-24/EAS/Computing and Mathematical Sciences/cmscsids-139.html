Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

Course content is interesting, probably would not have learned this anywhere else. Most of the homework is more or less hard algebra (i.e. proving bounds and such using probability and linear algebra), without much construction, it seems. (There are some constructive problems but not many.) The beginning homeworks are decently straightforward but become more complex as the course material becomes increasingly abstract as well.

Lecturer presents the material well, but the lecture notes seem to be even better, since the pacing can be a bit odd at times. Grading is very strange. Grading is very strict on some parts, for example in showing work (skipping somewhat trivial steps will take half the points off a problem), yet lenient in other parts. In particular, it is completely feasible to "fudge" a problem (perhaps there is a hole in your logic, etc.) and still get 100% of the points. Hopefully grading becomes more reasonable next time the class is offered, as that is my main gripe with the class. Otherwise if you are interested in advanced algorithms not typically taught in an introductory algorithms class, I think this is a good class.
I highly recommend going to lecture. Some of the lecture notes are not as helpful as lecture is for the sets. The sets can sometimes be challenging, so go to office hours if you get stuck since a lot of problems are solved with ideas that are hard to come up with.
