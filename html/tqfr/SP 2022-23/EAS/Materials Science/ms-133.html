Comments

Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? In particular, comments about workload/distribution of the workload of the course, the necessity of the textbook, unexpected time requirements or flexibility, or unspecified prerequisites could be particularly helpful.

Be prepared to invest way more time into this course outside of classes and homeworks than expected. Office hours and recitation sessions (totaling 6+ hours) were almost mandatory and the TA would criticize those who did not attend these. The textbooks were very helpful.
Depending on who your TA and professors are, be prepared for this class to become your focus for the term. I'd say attend all recitations/office hours, but you probably wouldn't get any research done.
The course will be hard, really hard, about one and a half times harder than you originally expected. At the same time, in reality, the topics you go through are interesting, but they are not many, and they will not occur in every research, let's be honest. I would not recommend this course if it is not core. And if it is core, good luck, you will need it.
Be ready to put the extra work, and make sure to READ THE TEXTBOOKS, because relying on the lectures/notes alone will not give you a satisfying level of understanding for this course. OH are valuable and should not be underestimated. I would recommend not to take very coursework/homework heavy courses along this one, because especially if you're seeing this content for the first time, it will take a lot of extra time every week to do well.
