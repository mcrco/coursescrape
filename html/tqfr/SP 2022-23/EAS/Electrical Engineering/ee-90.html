Comments

Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? In particular, comments about workload/distribution of the workload of the course, the necessity of the textbook, unexpected time requirements or flexibility, or unspecified prerequisites could be particularly helpful.

I just don't know how to feel about this class. This class is easily one of the most disorganized classes I have taken at this school, the TA's, professor, and students are almost never on the same page about anything. This much is clear, the blame for a lot of this classes' problems is not on Prof. Ohanian, but on the EE department itself. It is ridiculous we barely have any practical EE classes (or even an entire power electronics class, other colleges have entire tracks for this), but trying to put everything we need to know for our futures into one class produced terrible results. For example, we have one 1 hour lecture on oscillator circuits. We should have either learned this elsewhere or spent more time on this subject. I honestly learned more from Googling information. Teaching wise, this is not on Prof. Ohanian, but on how much needs to be covered. I would possible have the lectures focus more on circuit theory/noise and less on some of the other aspects (like the PCB manufacturing stuff) to really hone in on the design asepects.
This next line of thinking is going out to the EE department, who I'm not sure reads these. We have to PAY for our circuit components. Who in their right minds think that students, who already pay 80 k a year to attend this school, have to pay MORE out of pocket because the EE department "doesn't have any money." They do have money, they just don't have money for students by the look of it. Every other department pays for their projects, especially in a class that's required to graduate. And we wonder why no one is choosing EE anymore?
Back to core contents of the class, I do think the project is miserable. It is incredibly hard to hit specs and I do think the entire project needs to be overhauled. There was a general lack of enthusiasm to go through this class, another hurdle that needs to be jumped through to get our EE degrees. I think the project should be reworked to have options, include other design aspects possibly, etc. No one wanted to build a function generator. Secondly, the amount of writing in the class should be reduced. While strong communication skills are necessary, I do think some of the writing done was becoming a time sink.
Overall, for the other students, if you are taking this class, you are a EE and have no other option. It's going to be rough, but you will get through it. Rely on TA's and especially your other classmates. Don't procrastinate and take extra care in the design stage to make things work. This class is VERY back end heavy, so don't take any other project classes.
Overall, EE 90 was of the worst experiences for me at Caltech, but I'm not sure how much can be changed. This class makes me regret becoming a EE.

tldr to Prof. Ohanian: Change some of the project aspects as well as lecture structure. Professor and Students need to work better to change the EE curriculum as a whole to fix some of the core issues such as fitting a ton of necessary content into this class
tldr to students: Caffeine supply will come in handy.
This class has the potential to be really good. The project is fairly interesting and straightforward and the skills taught are absolutely essential for any EE: how to design, simulate, breadboard, test, use Altium, etc. It's rather unfortunate that no other classes in the department do this, and I really enjoyed EE 90 for its emphasis on practical project experience.

However, this class was probably one of the most poorly taught, poorly organized classes I've taken at Caltech. The professor told us that "disorganization is part of industry", but I'm not sure I've ever encountered this level of disorganization before. The TA's and professor were never on the same page about assignment deadlines or requirements, leading to conflicting answers to basic questions that ended up wasting a lot of time. Lectures just consisted of blocks of text on slides, with little to no explanation or derivation of important concepts, so most people stopped going after the first few weeks. The professor, too, was not helpful when students asked him questions: he either became combative, provided answers completely unrelated to the question asked, or sent links to generic websites that gave little information. This class is a requirement for the major, so it's not like this review will encourage anyone not to take the class, but I just want to emphasize that at its core EE 90 is an important, good class. It just needs a major overhaul in terms of the lecture format, teaching style, and organization.

And finally, this is not the professor or TA's fault, but the fact that we have to pay for our own parts given the fact that we pay $80,000 for tuition a year is ridiculous. I had to spend almost $100 of my own money on parts when other majors (namely, mechanical engineering) get their $1500 projects fully covered by the mechanical engineering department. I hope the EE department works to fix this as soon as possible.
The professor and future TAs need to do a lot of work before this course actually lives up to its potential. It can be such a good systems engineer class, but the organization was severly lacking. Prof. Ohanian said he's revamping it, so be ready to be rocked around. Just be on top of the weekly assignments and go to lecture to at least get a taste of EE hardware, if you're into that.
