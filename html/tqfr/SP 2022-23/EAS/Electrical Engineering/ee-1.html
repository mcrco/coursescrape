If you're looking just to complete your menu requirement this is a great class. Four essays (one every two weeks) that are 1-2 pages and graded very kindly. Basically a free 9 units and A.
EE1, boy where do I start. Also side note I have my EE1 essay due in like an hour so I'm going to go through this quick.

At the beginning of the term, lectures were online. This was boring, and the first homework assignment wasn't out until around midterms so the online videos were rarely watched.

Then out of nowhere, we were all blessed with an email saying that the man the myth the legend PPV would give an in-person lecture. I remember the face of me and my colleagues as we heard the news: pure delight. The day of that lecture I actually arrived late because I did not know where the room was but I digress. As I walked in the lecture hall I noticed most people were sitting in the back with an exponentially fewer amount sitting in the further rows. It wasn't first-row Friday so I decided to chill in the back with one of my homies.

The lecture soon started and PPV started by noting how most of us were sitting in the back. He said back in his day everyone would sit in the front, and he declared that next class everyone should fill in the front. Then PPV stated his 4 RULES for lecture which are as follows:
1. Sit in the front
2. Don't fall asleep
3. Pay attention to PPV
4. Don't spread misinformation, PPV doesn't bite!
Breaking any one of these rules is a crime against PPV so if you're reading this and plan to take EE1 FOLLOW THESE rules!!

Anyway back to my experience in the first lecture. After PPV laid out his rules, he started the lecture which was just a review of the online lecture video on music/harmonics. About 30 minutes into the lecture I looked to my right and saw my homie drifting into sleep. HE WAS BREAKING RULE #2. I quickly shook him awake to make sure he wouldn't get in trouble. Another 30 minutes passed and I soon drifted into a daydream. PPV's low soothing voice turned into a constant hum of harmony which relaxed every brain cell in me. The ambient orange surroundings of the lecture hall further took me deeper into a state of daydreaming. My eyelids felt like boat anchors, slowly lowering to solidify a sense of place in the dark unknown. Then somewhere in me I heard PPV say WAKE UP and suddenly I snapped out of this state. I had almost broken rule number 2 as well! I looked at my watch and saw that there were 5 minutes left of lecture. I had a class immediately after so I left slightly early.

I never attended another in-person lecture.

The class after this moment just involved me waiting for an essay assignment to be posted, then waiting until the day that essay was due, and then doing it in a couple hours. Actually, for the first essay I did something different. So here's what happened:

When the first essay got released, I looked at the assignment and saw the prompt. For honor code reasons I cannot say the prompt but to me, it was very doable given my very limited lecture attendance. However, one of the TAs told me that PPV was giving A+ to essays to about 10% of students. After hearing this I knew I had to pop off. I spent a solid couple of hours first understanding the prompt and then I went to work on my essay. I had started 2 days before the deadline to give me time to add in every bit of information as possible. Also sidenote, the only requirement for the essays are that they have to be 2 pages MAX. Thus with me putting images and adjusting the spacing and font size, I was able to fill up 2 pages. I looked over my essay multiple times to make sure it was something my grandmother would enjoy reading, as this was one of PPV's criteria. I then submitted the essay and awaited the grade.

Not sure how quickly the grade came back but it was before the second essay was due, which was in 2 weeks. The day the grade came out, I opened gradescope and saw 4/4.3, an A. At this moment I realized that I had to put even MORE work into my essay to get an A+. However, at some point I realized that I would rather put this time into my other classes to ensure that I get solid grades in those classes. Thus from then on, I put about a solid 2 hours into my EE1 essays, enough to get As throughout the 4 assignments.

Do I regret not going full sweat mode on each essay? Perhaps you would think yes, as after all A+>A, but at the end of the day, it's not the grade that matters, it's about truly understanding the material. And although I myself definitely did not understand all the material, there were some significant points about sampling frequency, Fourier analysis, and other topics that have stuck with me as I'm writing this and I will take this with me to the end! In light of this, try your best for the A+, but do not worry if you just get an A, there are more important things to take from this class than the "easy" grade.

As a parting message, "Work hard, you will be rewarded" - PPV
