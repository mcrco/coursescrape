As far as I know there is no textbook for the course. To any students who are considering taking the course, Office Hours are extremely helpful because sometimes there is so much material in Web Development, it is really hard to find the right way to do something and much easier if you ask someone who is more familiar with the subject. I found the initial parts of this course to be a reasonable amount of material, but struggled a bit keeping up with all the resources at the end of the class. Although, I think that is sometimes just the nature of teaching this topic, since there is so much to cover about the web and so little time during term. This year (Spring 2023), there were 4 creative projects (which you could develop your own website for as long as it satisfied certain requirements), 4 homeworks (1, 2a, 2b, 3, where I count both parts of the second homework as 2 homeworks), and 1 final project (with a final project proposal due midway through term). I found the homeworks very helpful to my understanding and the creative projects were fun to do. The final project was very rewarding since I got to watch so much come together. I would highly suggest taking this course if you are looking to do any sort of web development, since it will make your website more professional and easier to maintain. There are no prerequisites I can think of that would be necessary, but a good coding background would be helpful.
