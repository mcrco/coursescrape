This is a pretty difficult CS class - probably one of the three most difficult ones the CS option/minor requires you to take. However, as someone who took CS 21 as a freshman and CS 38 as a sophomore and having taken Ma 6a in between, I honestly found that CS 38 was nowhere near as difficult as its reputation implies. Do not be mistaken - you will spend at least 15 hours per set if you seek to complete every problem, and your schedule for spring term will essentially revolve around CS 38; however, if you accept these two truths, the class is actually quite manageable. The midterm is harder than the final, but with each of these assessments only constituting 15% of your grade, it is entirely possible to do poorly on these and do well on the sets and still end with a respectable grade. TA notes are exceptional for this class, and the notes and lecture slides combined are basically sufficient to answer most questions on the sets. Overall, this is a very difficult proof-based CS class that will be a huge time-suck each week, but it is very possible to do well in the class, especially with a couple of other hard proof-based CS/math classes under your belt.
