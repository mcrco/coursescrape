Comments

Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? In particular, comments about workload/distribution of the workload of the course, the necessity of the textbook, unexpected time requirements or flexibility, or unspecified prerequisites could be particularly helpful.

Workload is fair, if you have experience programming CS1 will be maybe 3-5 units max for you and you don't need to attend lectures. The grading is also fair and you're on pass fail anyways, but the assignments don't get graded until like 3 weeks later.
The class will not be too bad if you have previous experience in coding.
Attend lecture if you do not have prior/limited programming experience (they're also fun to attend as Hovik's live coding is very useful/engaging). Professor Hovik also sometimes gives solutions to the homework assignments in lectures, so don't count lectures completely out when you take this course. Start the assignments early and attend office hours throughout the week to get the MP's done before the due date. Labs were good, no midterm, and the final exam was a good test of the material learned thus far.
I felt that this course was pretty chill. As someone who had some experience with Python, I felt that the course content was easy to understand. For anyone who hasn't taken CS before, I'd highly recommend going through the readings as they do a good job of explaining content. If you get stuck, you could also check out javaTpoint, geeksforgeeks, and the freecodecamp Python beginner course on YouTube (it explains OOP pretty well). The TAs for this course are truly amazing and El does a great job at explaining concepts and helping students with the mini-projects. They're also pretty reasonable so if you're having trouble with the course, don't be afraid to reach out. I'd also recommend going to the office hours hosted by El and the TAs. Even if you haven't started the MP (weekly mini-project) yet, it's a chill environment to get work done in. Also, the labs aren't something to worry about. At first, I was a little hesitant about the fact that we'd have to complete an assignment in 1 hour but the work is pretty reasonable (fill in the blanks). Lastly, lecture attendance pretty much dies down towards the end of the class but I'd recommend going to
1. Get re-work tokens that you can use to re-do an MP for a higher grade
2. Ask El about topics in CS like current research, Python libraries, working in industry and impostor syndrome.
CS 1 was a good class, especially with this term's small size. Professor El Hovik was very passionate about the material, and they always were willing to share fun things about CS with us. The mini-projects (i.e., homework) and labs might not have been all that interesting from a programming perspective, but they were useful for learning how to use the coding we learned in practical situations and they were usually useful, cute, and/or satisfying. The final was a little long, but I really appreciated that it was unlimited time, and I realize the length was necessary to make it (very) comprehensive. However, the grading was very slow, and most of my work was never(!) graded.
