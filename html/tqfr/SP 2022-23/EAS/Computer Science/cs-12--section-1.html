Comments

Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? In particular, comments about workload/distribution of the workload of the course, the necessity of the textbook, unexpected time requirements or flexibility, or unspecified prerequisites could be particularly helpful.

This course is a great introduction to the implementation of neural networks "from scratch" (using TensorFlow rather than Keras), which is useful in understanding the underlying mechanisms as well as in implementing novel models for research. The topics are chosen very well in terms of relevance, including sections on adversarial attacks and Word2Vec. The only real prerequisite is a basic understanding of object-oriented programming in Python (at the level of CS 1). Familiarity with matrix multiplication also helps with ensuring correct dimensions of weight matrices and such. More work is required at the beginning of the course to process data and define the model architecture for the first time, but this code can be easily reused for the later labs.
Great course! Amazing TAs!
