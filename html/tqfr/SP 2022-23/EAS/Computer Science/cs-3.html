CS 3 is a very fun class, as long as you have a good group, which I luckily did. I really appreciate Adam Blank's style of lectures and course organization, so I really enjoyed CS 3. C is tough to get a hold on at the beginning, but once you figure out the semantics of the language, it's fun to use. The physics engine projects can be frustrating at times but getting them to work felt satisfying and the demos were cool.

I like the game project a lot as well. It's really the first time in the CS major that you get to just make something of your own accord, and using the engine you've built over the term feels really cool. Plus, it's a nice hefty project you could possibly showcase in your resume.

I will say that I think I'd prefer a lab component to the course like in CS 2 more than the practicum assignments in CS 3. While the regex portion of practicum was fine, it could be hard to gauge exactly what the graders were looking for in terms of the code quality assignments. I wish the desired magnitude of the changes/types of changes were a little more clear, but that could just be a "I can't read properly" problem.

Adam is very good at choosing TAs. My group's TA this term was Sarah, and she was absolutely fantastic. She was always super duper helpful and knew how to do/fix everything all the time. She answered messages very frequently and also hosted office hours frequently. Sarah was also very patient and kind, and never made me feel bad about asking a seemingly dumb question. She definitely improved my experience in CS 3.

CS 3 is a lot of work, but it's pretty enjoyable (at least for me, a CS major) and is very satisfying.
