This course is very manageable. It is asynchronous and is mostly dependent on your presentations throughout the term. I am still confused as to how the grading is broken down. I would start working early with your group and set up a consistent working schedule / work division.
This course would be very time-consuming if one really wants to learn and build a model with a decent performance.
It offers a challenging project with great flexibility, one could implement any ML model and could arrange his time however he wants.
For me, it is very valuable, since it would be very difficult for one to find a course elsewhere, providing such extensive datasets and offering Caltech HPC to use.
However, I don't recommend taking it if one is not prepared to put effort/weight in it. The seats are already very full.
This is a project-based class that is an option for fulfilling the AI/ML track of the CS major at Caltech. I found this class quite manageable, although we managed to avoid some problems which other groups had a lot of trouble with (ex: we had our own GPU, while other groups had to use HPC). the TAs essentially fully run this class, and they are very kind and responsive. The class itself is also very relaxed - the three required presentations over the course of the term are very chill, and there is very little pressure to actually perform well, as the TAs emphasize that your leaderboard ranking contributes to only a small portion of your overall grade. This class also seemed much easier than CS 148/CS 159 also offered this term (which some students took as an alternative to CS 156b), so if difficulty is a consideration, I would strongly recommend this class.
This is a good course if you have a good group. Not a ton of work, but just a tip, start the code a little early so you don't run into long queues the week before presentation.

I thought this course was pretty cool; it's essentially a term-long ML research project. The course is as good as the amount of time you can put into it. An consequence of this is that this affects people taking 38 at the same time. Thus, I honestly think you can get more out of the course (as a CS major doing the "normal" sophomore schedule) by pushing 156b off to your junior year. Having the chance to gain more experience in ML and having more time really increases the learning benefit in this class imo.
This class was not a huge commitment for me in the end. The whole class is only a single project, and it was enough for my team to get a good grade by doing only a few hours of research/coding on the project per week. This class stressed me out the entire term, though, as it was not made clear to me what exactly we needed to accomplish or how the class would be graded. I would recommend doing very good research as the majority of the grade is on presentation and not performance, and the actual code was less emphasized in this class.
The class is essentially a 10-week long project in teams of 5. Choose your group ahead of time to make your life easier. This year, we were given a dataset of lung x-rays and were trying to identify certain pathologies.

The requirements are extremely vague, so you can put as much or as little time into it as you want. This made the class feel much more unsatisfying, as my group did a lot of the work sporadically right before the deadlines. If you're looking to learn more ML concepts third term, I would recommend taking cs159 instead. However, the class is a fun time and an "easy A", so if you need a break from an otherwise difficult schedule, I would highly recommend.
Great class to take along with CS38. Completely self-paced, just make sure to get your models working and spend enough time :), overall really solid class.
Super chill class, basically like a research project that is done with a group and with a cool topic that is designated by the instructors. Make sure to plan things big picture-wise and goals-wise in the beginning, as it is possible to just slack off and not make significant progress if you are unclear of the goals of the week.
Also as it is a group project, make sure to get with a group that you will work well with. We individually worked on small segments of the big project then came together to discuss, help troubleshoot or debug together, etc, which worked well.
