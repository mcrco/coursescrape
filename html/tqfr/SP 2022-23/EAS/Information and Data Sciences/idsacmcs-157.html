As always Professor Zuev is an amazing lecturer and really cares about his students. His lectures are clear and interesting. This was another classic Zuev class with the 60%-20%-20% problem set-midterm-final grade breakdown, lowest problem set dropped, and MATLAB problems on the sets. I really like this grade breakdown, drop policy, and the MATLAB problems really help to solidify understanding. I also liked how he posted comprehensive notes and lecture videos which are really useful summaries and good to review before the exams.

I thought the midterm was the exact right difficulty. The final was harder than I thought it would be for the 3 hour time given. In the future i think an easier exam might be better, but it was not too bad.

In terms of the material, I would recommend this class if you want a real in-depth understanding of the concepts covered in high school AP Stats and want to learn how statistical inference is actually used in science and engineering. It also serves as a great intro class before you go into machine learning, statistical learning, and AI. I learned a lot from the content of the class.

I would highly recommend this class if you want to learn the real intuition behind what you learned in AP Stats and learn how to use statistics in science and engineering! Also Zuev is just awesome so that's another perk :) !

IDS 157, in my opinion, teaches you statistics from the very foundation. What was glossed over as "1-prop-z-test" or "t-test" in AP statistics is actually explained thoroughly in this class. The notes were amazing (almost like a textbook), and the TAs are very helpful.

Ever wonder why a term 1/(n-1) is added to the front of sample variance? Or, what happens if you can't make any assumptions about the normality of the sampled data?
