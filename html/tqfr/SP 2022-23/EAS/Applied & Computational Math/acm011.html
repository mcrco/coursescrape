Work includes one set every other week which takes around 6 hours to complete and weekly in-class assignments. The class is very fun and taught well. The TA's are very helpful and you get a good grasp on matlab.
This was an excellent class! At 6 units, it is a fairly-united class, as homework assignments are generally biweekly and thus you have plenty of time to work on a given homework assignment. This class is a great way to become familiar with MATLAB, so if that's a skill you want to develop, ACM 11 is a great way to start. There's a decent amount of overlap content-wise with classes such as Ma 2, CS 156a, and CS 155, so if you've taken any of these classes before, you'll probably find a lot of the content in this class redundant (however, those classes aren't done in MATLAB, so you'd learn cool MATLAB tricks in this one to do the same things). Overall, I highly recommend this class, especially if your major has any sort of ACM breadth requirement.

ACM 11 is a very interesting course. I would highly recommend it if you have room in your schedule that you are trying to fill. The focus of this course is understanding the computational/mathematical methods, so there is a lot of theoretical material that you will have to absorb. However, taking this class does give you a very good practical introduction to MATLAB as well. Learning MATLAB was my main motivation for taking this course in the first place, and I am very satisfied with the outcome. I feel like I have learned lots of cool things about MATLAB in addition to just being introduced to its basic syntax. That being said, this definitely is not a class that you can just push aside. The homework assignments were very reasonable, but I would recommend starting them a bit early. They are very insightful, in that you will be able to learn the concepts as you go along in the assignment, but to make the most out of them, you might have to take a bit of time. Finally, Professor Baptista is a very excellent professor. They truly do care about their students and they have a really great teaching style. All in all, if you're debating whether or not to take this class, as long as you have no strong reason not to, I would highly recommend taking this class.
