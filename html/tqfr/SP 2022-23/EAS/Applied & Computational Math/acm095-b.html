There is an option to calculate your grade based on problem sets/exam performance with a pretty normal grade weighting, but there is a second option to calculate your grade based on only your midterm and final grades with 50-50 weighting (the higher of the two is taken to be your actual grade). This means that some people don't do any of the sets at all and choose to only complete the midterm and final, in which case this course is pretty light! I did not do this; there are 6 problem sets, a midterm and final exam. Each set typically has 4 questions, with 2 of them being very straightforward and direct applications of techniques described in the lecture notes, 1 introducing some new concept and walking you through the steps of it, and 1 that is probably more confusing that will require some help in office hours. The lecture notes are very thorough and read in an intuitive almost storytelling manner of derivations, but can be hard to skip through if you are just trying to look for the right equation. I took this course after 95a to supplement physics/astro core, and it definitely helped clarify some concepts around the wave equation from Ph 12b/2b that are kind of just handed to you in the physics course. I would highly recommend this course to fulfill the scientific elective course for physics/astro majors.
Go to Office hours!! The lectures are not as phenomenal as Zuevs 95a lectures (how I wish) but make use of office hours to allow you to fully understand what is expected of you!
