The class was generally interesting but the requirement for coming to in person lecture in addition with an unforgiving late policy made managing it around all the other ME core classes third term extremely difficult. As a result I will be performing worse in this class than any of the other 11ab classes solely due to late penalties + participation. Despite this, I thought most of the material in the course was interesting. For students taking this class in the future I would recommend starting on sets early so you can go to office hours and ask questions. Additionally, getting a deans tutor may not be a bad idea if you are struggling to understand the concepts.
This class is much more math-intensive than ME11a/b which can be a steep learning curve especially since a lot of the new math is front-loaded into weeks 1 and 2 with Navier-Stokes. Furthermore, lectures tended to be very derivation heavy. However, they improved and became a bit more balanced with conceptual knowledge after week 4 or so. One thing to note is that the late policy can be crippling. We got 5 free late days (to clarify the late days are not counted as 5 x 24 hrs, but rather 1 min late on a set = 1 day), but every late day past these 5 is a 2% cut to your total grade up to a 10% maximum cut. It's a much harsher penalty than what we had previously in 11/12ab and something to be vigilant about.

The course, while an intro to fluid mechanics did not cover alot on Bernoulli's equation and different variations thereof. However, the course went through the Navier Stokes equations and its different forms and applications. As someone who only took this course in the Me 11 series, I did not expect heat equations to come up but it was taught well without needing prior knowledge. Heat equations made sense here because of convection.
Unfamiliar tensor math and PDEs may make this course seem daunting at first, but realizing that for the types of flows being studied in the course there exist known solutions about their velocity profiles, etc. will help you work around the math and kind of sanity check your results. Taking 95b concurrently also helps make some of the PDE work easier.
