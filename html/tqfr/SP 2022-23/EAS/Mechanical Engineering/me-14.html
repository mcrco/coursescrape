Comments

Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? In particular, comments about workload/distribution of the workload of the course, the necessity of the textbook, unexpected time requirements or flexibility, or unspecified prerequisites could be particularly helpful.

This class was overall very informative and modeled wonderfully after the workforce in my opinion. The first half of the term is dedicated to set-based homeworks, but beware of the second half where you have both the transmission project and the mechatronics project (~ 2 weeks each). For the transmission project, you're restricted to milling and lathing (no 3D printing or waterjetting) which made the project very time consuming. The mechatronics project grants free reign in terms of designing and operations allowed (so long as they're done in machine shop or the techlab) which is both a blessing and a curse. There are also several benchmark assignments due throughout these projects (PDR, CDR, BOM, Drawing Files, Major Fabrication Milestone, Demo Day) which roll out very quickly. Extensions also aren't granted generously so be sure to be on top of your work.
This course is very interesting and hands-on. It is a big time commitment, but you learn a lot. It made my term very overwhelming to balance with four other classes, so just expect to commit a large chunk of time towards this course.
Really fun class, however very very stressful. It moves fast paced and is a little over ambitious. Good for introduction of design however it is useful to come in with some knowledge of design and solidworks.
The final project timeline is pretty rushed. I spent like 50 hours on the project in the shop alone during the last two weeks of the class. It's a lot of work, but you can do it! I had a great time.
It was a fun class but had a LOT of work(up to 20 hrs/week) expected at the end of term
Be prepared to never eat, sleep, or feel peace of mind!
