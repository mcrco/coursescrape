Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? In particular, comments about workload/distribution of the workload of the course, the necessity of the textbook, unexpected time requirements or flexibility, or unspecified prerequisites could be particularly helpful.

This was the most organized of the Ph 12abc series (though this may vary with instructors). The problem sets are typically 4-5 questions with all but one of the questions being pulled from the textbook K&K. Prof. Patterson does an excellent job of adhering to the notation and nomenclature used in the textbook, so it is easy to switch between attending lecture and reading the textbook if necessary. The midterm and final is pretty similar to the problem sets, but usually tests your conceptual understanding of stat mech concepts far more heavily than textbook problems that are solved in sets; be sure to attend lecture for more clarification of the conceptual applications of the topics each week. Go to lecture!! It will save you!! Sets only took me > 4-6 hours when I skipped lecture that week. There is a tiny bit of content from Ph 12b and Ma 3 that might help you in this course (fermions/bosons, counting probability), but if you don't take Ph 12b/2b you will be totally fine in Ph 12c.
Excellent lectures, reasonable homework and exams
well taught & properly united; highly recommend taking this class! :D
I really enjoyed this course for both the material and the instructor. Prof. Patterson is a great lecturer. His pace is reasonable and it is not difficult to follow the entire lecture. He posts lecture notes, but they're not super neat or detailed, so I'd recommend going to lecture as often as possible.
As a non-physics major, I actually really enjoyed this class. The material is interesting, and Patterson is really thorough in his lectures and engaging with the content. The sets are reasonable, although I will mention that they get longer/tougher as the course progresses (specifically after the midterm). The midterm was very fair, but I definitely found the final harder. However, it is still very doable and the grading for this class is really nice! I would definitely recommend for non-physics majors to take this class as opposed to Ph2c for the core requirement.
