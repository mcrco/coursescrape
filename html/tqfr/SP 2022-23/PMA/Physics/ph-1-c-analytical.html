Ph1c was a very difficult course. It is for sure a step up from Ph1b (at least for me) and you really do need to work hard in this class. As for Professor Hsieh, he is a phenomenal professor, honestly the best that I have had at Caltech so far. He does a great job at explaining concepts and his lectures were always very well done. When you leave Professor Hsieh's lectures, you really do feel as though you have learned something. Not only that, but the content is very interesting. Combine interesting content with good teaching, and you get this class. To give you an example, one lecture Professor Hsieh finally connected special relativity and magnetism, showing how we can derive magnetism from the principles of special relativity. The content of that lecture was so interesting and Professor Hsieh did such a great job at presenting it that the whole class couldn't help but to applaud a great lecture. Additionally, Professor Hsieh is very open and responsive to questions, and very accessible outside of class.

The homeworks are definitely much more time-consuming and require a lot more thought to go into them. I would definitely recommend starting the homework sets early so that you have time to actually think through them, go to office hours for help, and work with other students. Going through them does help you learn the material, which is nice, but there is still lots of thinking that needs to go into them.

The quizzes were pretty difficult in my opinion. They were definitely a step up from the homework and I would highly recommend studying for them and just giving it your all.

The book, Purcell, is probably one of your greatest assets in this class. It is honestly a very good book, which goes through both rigorous mathematical derivation as well as physical intuition. I did my best to keep up with the readings posted on the class calendar, making sure to at least read all the chapters that were listed before the next quiz I took. Reading the book proved to be very crucial for this class. It goes into much more detail that you really need to get the homeworks done. If you haven't already, I would recommend reading the book.

Ultimately, this class was for sure a step up from 1b analytical. It is definitely a time consumer, so that is something to keep in mind when planning out your schedule. If you're interested in physics, definitely consider this class, but be prepared for the workload to come.
