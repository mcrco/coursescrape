The course is fine, I wish there could be curve tho; I risked to take it on grades and was kind of scared of final grade; I get a full score for both BOE quizzes, but I guess I kind of do bad for final; so end up getting A-. But yeah, it's not a bad course.
This class was a little more intense than Ph 1b. The class is weighted 30% homework, 30% BoEs (BoE quizzes included in this grade), and 40% final. I initially took this course on P/F because of the final, but I'm glad I switched to grades, since for me, grasping the material was easier than I thought it would be.

The first half of the class focused on magnetism and AC Circuits, and magnetism was covered on the first BoE quiz. When I was in high school, these concepts were taught to me in my AP Physics C class, so this part was a review, so I didn't study for the first BoE Quiz

The second half of the class delves into waves, optics, and special relativity, which I had no prior experience in. However, to solve the homework, I just had to use some intuition and then find the correct formula in the textbook to get the solution. The OOMs from Ph 1b Practical come back, under the name BoE. If you find a good TA that walks with you through the problem, you are guaranteed to get full credit on the weekly BoE sessions. However, I did have to study for BoE Quiz 2 to ensure I fully understood the material.

The final is very relaxed. There were six questions and we just had to pick four of them to solve. I would say that this final was much easier than the Ph 1b final.

The homework assignments are all placed on the calendar ahead of time, so pace yourself as you wish to.
