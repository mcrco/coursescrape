This course honestly wasn't too bad. Sure it's a bit of work, but honestly, it's a fair amount. The course (at least I felt) was pretty well paced. Of course, it does pick up after the midterm once we get into multivariable integration and the vector calculus theorems, but still manageable. One piece of advice I can give is to pay close attention to the conditions of the theorems written in the book before you invoke those theorems. Yes, this is the practical class, so you there is not a lot that you need to formally "prove" but do remember to at least remark that the conditions for the proofs you are using are met before invoking them, that is pretty important.
Other than that, the lectures were pretty well done as well, Professor Flach does a good job at presenting material and explaining the theory behind it. Also the textbook is a really great resource. I found it especially helpful in internalizing concepts and helping me with the homework, so make sure to use it.
Other than that, like I said, this is a very fair class, as it can definitely be a bit challenging at times, but overall, nothing extremely crazy.
