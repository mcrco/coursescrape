If you have prior linear algebra experience, this class should be quite easy for you (sets usually took less than 1 hour for me); if not, you might have to put in a bit of extra work (2-3 hours maybe?), but even so, the class is very manageable. The sets are of reasonable length (4 questions a week from the textbook and problems the professor writes); the questions on the sets are straightforward computational questions (no proofs). Quizzes are every couple of weeks and are also reasonable; they are similar in format (and conceptually) to problem set questions, so make sure you really understand how to do the questions on pset. If you do well enough on sets and quizzes over the term, you don't have to take the final, which is really nice. If you need help understanding concepts, go to Philip's recitation, he is very helpful.
Lectures felt not very helpful, but definitely attend recitation and use the textbook as a reference for definitions and formulas you need to know. I came in without any background in linear algebra and am not a super math-oriented person, but this course was not bad at all and took a very reasonable amount of time work-wise, which was nice coming off of a term of Ma 1a. You can be excused from taking the final exam if you have an overall grade of 85% or higher, so I definitely recommend putting effort into the problem sets and staying on top of the content to prepare for quizzes so that you can have one less final to take.
