The assignments were very easy and uninteresting. One positive is that the instructors used Piazza, which allows students to anonymously ask questions.

Overall this was a very very disappointing class, and this comes from a person not interested in statistics and probability, as in I did not have high expectations. This will be an easy grade if that's your primary concern, but if you care about learning and receiving useful information, I would recommend taking Ma140 instead.
This course was very badly taught and prepared, in my opinion. It lacked a lot of rigour. I did not attend any of the lectures due to course conflicts, but I can say that the lecture notes were hastily-prepared Google slides that were far from self-contained (e.g.: with screenshots from Wikipedia, graphs that lacked axis titles, an overall lack of flow, few rigourous mathematical definitions (event space was even wrongly defined)).

The problem sets started off with some somewhat nontrivial counting problems that could fill up a page or two of computation but then went to being HS-esque plug and chug statistics questions. More shockingly, 2 out of the 8 sets (a quarter!) were dominated by questions of the type "Describe this graph. Can you name its variables? Are the axes labelled? Was it nicely chosen?" (yes, I think everyone got full marks. Also, this choice of set presented itself as very inconsistent with the fact that the lecture slides' figures were so badly presented, lacking titles and axis labels, in light of the fact that we were given a whole set on what makes a graph "good", being asked the same 5 questions for a total of 6 graphs).

The organisation of the course further seemed bad, and the grading scheme is not communicated either. I was hopeful for the Piazza discussions page that was set up by the instructors. However, when I first logged on a little after around the midterm period, I was only faced with the disappointing reality that questions from weeks ago had remained unanswered. Funnily, for questions that were addressed, there were even misunderstandings between the instructor and the TA with regards to the set-up of the problem.

Probabilities and statistics remain rich and important subjects in mathematics. It is a pity that we did not get more out of this Core course. I hope these issues will be resolved for future students that deserve the education that we did not get.
