Ma 1b-an and Ma 1b-prac follow a very similar structure, and honestly I would recommend analytical. It goes at a slightly slower pace than practical.
As an applied physics major, I found Math 1b to be excellent. We learned about the fundamentals of linear algebra starting with vector spaces, span, linear dependence, dimension to linear transformations, matrices, null space, rank nullity theorem, invertibility, determinants, eigenvalues/vectors, diagonalization, and inner products, self adjoint matricies. Professor Graber is very knowledgeable and the lectures are clear and easy to follow. Doing the psets was also fun as the problems were the right difficulty. Note there is no midterm for the class but instead biweekly quizzes.
This class is most definitely for people who prefer working with math analytically rather than computationally.
Ma1b is the easiest of the Ma1 sequence. With any prior linear algebra experience, this class should be pretty chill and easy. On the other hand, this class generally lacks computation of linear algebra and the computational side of things are generally done quickly (if you haven't taken linear algebra, this may be a slight problem). Overall, decent class, Graber lectures are decent.
