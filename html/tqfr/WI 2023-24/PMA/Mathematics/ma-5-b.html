Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

Ma5b (and 5c) are definitely a step-up in difficulty from Ma5a. It was challenging in my opinion: there was a lot of content (it will give you tons of basics and techniques which are very useful in any algebra course/research) and Aluffi teaches fast (I believe he teaches the same course at Florida St. with 1.5 hour lectures). Aluffi is a great professor, but moves quite fast. However, his lecture notes and textbook are great, so you can generally catch up on anything you missed with those resources. Overall, with a more challenging math course like 5b, it will definitely help substantially boost mathematical maturity as well.
I was a huge fan of Prof. Aluffi over a yr before taking this class. The problem sets were really fun and well-curated for someone like me who loves messing around with category theory a lot. I definitely enjoyed working through the material.
Ma5b is a superb class that anyone interested in abstract algebra should take. Rings and modules are absolutely fundamental to higher math, and taking this class will provide a significant step up in your understanding of algebra. Not only will you be well-practiced in the basics of rings and modules, you will also become well acquainted with connections to other areas of math, from linear algebra (of which we do a fair amount - if you are a frosh, taking this course simultaneously with Ma1b will enrich your experience) to category theory / homological algebra.

Aluffi is a phenomenal lecturer and the class is very well structured. The workload in the class is quite reasonable, and the class has policies designed to help people in case they fall behind.
This course is a ton of fun! It gives you a look into the beautiful world of algebraic geometry and commutative algebra, and it teases at homological algebra. Highly recommend this course. It is a bit of work, but definitely worth it.
