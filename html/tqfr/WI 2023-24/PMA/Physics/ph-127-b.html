Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

This is a fantastic course, especially for students interested in theoretical condensed matter physics, but also generally for stat mech knowledge that any physicist should have. Prof Motrunich cares deeply about making sure that students are engaged and understand the material, and is clearly passionate when lecturing. The workload is fairly standard, and the homeworks ranged from taking 2-5 hours per week, and followed directly from lecture material. I highly recommend this course (and 127a) as a physics elective.
You should take this course if ur interested in stat. phys and stat field theory. It's very interesting and I think its presented well. Make sure you understand 127a well before though.
This class will build on the knowledge of Ph127a but will also involve a lot of combinatorial thinking and visualizations. There are a wide variety of techniques that are helpful especially if you want to pursue condensed matter physics in the future. The homeworks are quite reasonable and relavant to the course material, and you give a final presentation in place of final exam.
127b is not as logically connected as 127a. It feels more like an assortment of special topics in stat mech. It is hard sometimes, but the lecture notes are superb and the homework is almost always well designed to help you learn even more. Lesik is a great lecturer but this term I always feel like we are behind schedule a little. Still, great class and interesting topics!
This class adds onto Ph 127a and introduces students to topics integral to quantum phase transitions. Students should take this class if they want to learn about simple quantum phases that can be understood through methods such as majorana fermions and Landau-Ginsburg theory. This class was extremely helpful to gain the know-how to read papers on different lattice models and how to approach the broader subject. The class consists of weekly assignments as well as a final 20 minute presentation of the student's choosing. I would highly recommend this class to students interested in condensed matter/quantum materials. This class is not easy though.
Go to TA hours and form a study group

This is one of the best physics courses that I've taken at Caltech. The topics and techniques you learn here are really unlike anything else in the physics major requirements. It blew my mind just how CREATIVE the math gets. It's beautiful.

The course is also challenging, but in the best way possible. All the tools needed are explained very well in lecture, and covered in detail in the lecture notes. There are also no exams, only a final presentation, which helped reduce stress during finals week.
