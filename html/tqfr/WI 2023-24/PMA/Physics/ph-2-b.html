What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

Don't take this class for core req. Too hard for non-physics major
If you are taking this class, chances are that it is required. While the content itself is interesting enough to not be completely boring, the sets are simply a computational bash that have little to no relevance outside of making sure you know how to take an integral if Ma 1a did not already teach you.

The quizzes are not even remotely comparable to the sets, which test the conceptual nuances of quantum mechanics that never seem to be covered in the lecture notes nor in the homework. This issue would have been alleviated entirely if there were conceptual questions on the homework as well to assess our understanding. The only positive side of this class was that Brad was willing to accommodate as the class indicated struggle with the first couple of sets.
Note that with Professor Filippone Physics 2B resembles a similar format to Physics 12B with less derivation. Also, look at the textbook and go to recitations!

Bradley Filippone is great for this class!
The class itself is exactly what the course name and description say it is. Prof. Filippone is a good lecturer and tries to engage the class in his lectures. He answers questions and is very responsive to student feedback--moreso than most other faculty I've had experience with at Caltech!

Just a note: sometimes the homeworks can be imbalanced and read the course policy on the usage of software! Math heavy sets can get you bogged down in spending a lot of time doing calculation. Watch out for that. Use software where you can to save time. On the same note, sometimes sets took >6 hours and sometimes they took <6 hours. Start early and figure out which type of set it is each week. Sometimes questions had answers that were not from lecture, or the book, or were easily accessible on internet research. Ask questions in this case and ask TAs questions early so you don't have to use extensions or sleep days.
Try to take the course with peers you can collaborate with if at all possible. Trying to get through the material with only limited collaboration and OHs could be overwhelming.

The textbook is really great and the author has an entertaining voice, which is refreshing for a physics textbook.
