Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

This course is advertized by other students as an extremely time-consuming one, and although it does take up some time, it is not as bad. With the exception of the first experiment, everything was great.
Unlike other people, I believe that this class is reasonably united, you spend 1hr preparing for your recitation, 15mins in your recitation, 3h30mins in your lab and about 4hr for your report. Some of the conversations you will have with Prof. Politzer and Rice will give you incredible insights into the physics of the phenomenon we are analyzing.

The setups are exceptionally easy to handle, the amount of work put in the back-end is insane, all you usually have to do is just turn some knobs and you have your data. The experiment notes are great too, accessible level and much harder versions also given in the appendix, you will know everything you need to by the time you sit down and start the experiment. The recitations are very chill, the analysis grading is pretty lenient, the only thing is you will probably start off pretty badly grades wise but once you get a hang of it, this is a very manageable class.

Unfortunately, you will lose a lot of marks not due to the physics involved but trivial stuff, but you will still learn a lot. The one piece of advice I would give when you are drafting your lab report is to keep it concise and try to type it up and that helps remove a lot of redundant stuff from your analysis. The shorter your analysis the less likely it is that your TA misses interesting observations in your report.

I suggest these following experiments

1. Mossbauer Effect
2. Zeeman Effect
3. Delay Line
4. Kelvin Absolute Voltmeter

The sound waves in a cavity experiment is especially hard to analyze, I would avoid it.
The experiments in Ph 6 are interesting and tie in well with the theory taught in other Ph courses at Caltech. Additionally, the experience of having to be quizzed during recitation, needing to figure out explanations for why your data didn't exactly match what you expected from theory, etc. is useful. However, the grading for this class is very non-transparent, and varies between sections greatly. Additionally, the time requirement for Ph 6 is huge and I would definitely not recommend taking Ph 6 simultaneously with any other lab course (or any other similarly time-demanding course).
This is a fun lab class, but the overall workload in the 6 weeks when we have labs for this class is a bit over 9 hours. Being prepared before recitation and lab sessions is important for completing the experiments without being too confused. The instructors and TAs are always helpful and would answer any questions you have. The workload also depends on which lab you choose. The ones with relatively lighter write-ups are exp 7, 16, 12, 19, 25a.
This course is definitely quite heavy on work, and the pre-labs can be quite long and sometimes hard, but if you give yourself enough time to think through the lab, the lab itself is quite doable, and you have plenty of help during lab. If you get stuck in lab, try for a while, but it's definitely worth it asking a TA/prof so that you can move on. Definitely one of the best classes I've taken in terms of connecting physics topics I've seen so far in all my undergraduate classes, and I can see how this class will be helpful for my future, especially for experimental physics.
This was by far my favorite class I took this term. I found (almost) all of the labs to be interesting and, though some were a bit difficult with the prelabs, very rewarding. I realized that I really like error analysis and experimental design from this class and the TAs were extremely helpful. Please do not let the first lab assignment scare you, I was extremely stressed after Experiment 2 and it made week 2 or whenever it was due my worst week of the entire term by far, but it gets much better afterwards. Definitely leave yourself enough time to do the prelabs, and I would suggest typing your assignments rather than handwriting them as it is much quicker. Be prepared to spend a lot of time on this class but it is worth it if you like experimental physics!
