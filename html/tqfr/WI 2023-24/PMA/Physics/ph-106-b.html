Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

This class is very well structured and organized thanks to Prof. Golwala. His lectures are fast-paced but informative and worth attending. His lecture notes are also very comprehensive so you can learn just as much without attending lecture. Topics follow Ph 1bc very closely, but obviously go into far more depth and Prof. Golwala does a very good job of balancing review of Ph 1bc and introduction of new topics. Attending all of the weekly problem sessions gives you a +/- sign grade boost and they are very low-pressure sessions, so it's worth trying to attend all of them!
Not as bad as I expected. Challenging of course, but not as horrible as I feared. Go to lecture, study the examples in the lecture notes, take advantage of all office hours, and start the sets early, and you'll be fine. Ask lots of questions.
Midterm grading was brutal for me though, I hope they curve it.
Overall this is an excellent course that
1) Gave me a mature and intuitive understanding of electrostatics and magnetostatics (106c covers electrodynamics)
2) Really helped me hone my vector calculus and separation of variables skills (even if (1) didn't apply, this course would still be worth it for the math skills you obtain)

The key concepts covered were
- Overview of electric field, scalar potential, capacitance and potential energy
- Green functions, method of images, multipole expansions
- Solving Laplace's equation using SoV in Cartesian and spherical coordinates both in vacuum and in matter
- Magnetic field, vector potential and magnetic multipole expansions
- Solving magnetic boundary value problems in vacuum


Here's some points and suggestions
- Ph106a is not required. For non-Physics grad students (I am in EE), you should have taken at least one term of vector-based electromagnetics in undergrad.
- Problem sets are manageable, but I did need to go to office hours for more than half of the sets. Plan accordingly.
- Exams are not just repeats of the problem sets. In general, the exam problems are shorter, but require that you look at the problem in the right way if you want to avoid hours worth of algebra. Reviewing the previous year's exams really helped me since a lot of the key concepts are reused.
- You get 1 "free" homework extension per term. I'd recommend saving it for problem set 5. That set was the most time consuming in my opinion and I was happy to have an extra week to complete it.
- Attending weekly 1hr problem sessions gives you roughly 3% extra credit if you only miss one. However, these sessions were not super useful. If you're taking the class P/F, I wouldn't bother attending.
- Buy Griffith's if you don't have it (4th edition is what the course used, but any edition works since the problem sets are provided separately from the book). It's a great book for developing your intuition of electromagnetics.
- Consulting Jackson was helpful for at least 2 of the problem sets. This course follows Griffiths, but many of the proofs and derivations draw from Jackson since Griffiths can be very hand-wavey.
106B is a lot of work. That is not to say that the material is any worse than 106a, just that the nature of the calculations and derivations sometimes require a lot of careful tracking. This will be a useful course for anyone in physics, as a lot of mathematical formalisms are readdressed and you will develop an intuition in applying them. If you are a traditional physics student, then the 125b and 106b combination will be heavy. Please consider taking light hums and/or easier electives if you can. If you read the material well, keep on track with the lectures, you will be fine. Good luck!
Stay focused, go to lecture and OH, and don't get behind. As long as you prioritize not getting behind in this class, you'll manage fine more or less. Final overall grade is curved.
This class is difficult by nature but is valuable. Not the worst class I've had to take here as a physics major, even though I was told it would be one of the worst. Honestly, other than the exams, which were pretty fair just tough, it was pretty smooth sailing. The Friday problem set deadline can be stressful, especially if you have other pset due dates surrounding it, but it's ok.
Solid course in electro/magneto statics. The lectures are amazing! There is a lot of intuition that Professor Golwala explains that isn't in the lectures notes.
It is not a particularly easy course, but with enough effort you can ace it for sure
