Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

This is primarily a reading-based course. In it, you will delve into a wide array of topics in 3D deep learning and get a deep feel for where the field is going.
Learning how to read paper efficiently is important
Must-take for anybody interested in Computer Vision, learning, 3D. Very fun
Amazing class if you are interested in vision, or just want to learn more about what research/intellectual progression is like in deep learning. Apparently super helpful if you do/plan to do research in adjacent areas, but even if not you get great guidance on how to read/evaluate research papers, which is a valuable skill across disciplines.
Georgia is a great lecturer. This was a paper reading course, which was composed of 2 student-led presentations in each class. We were required to give 4 presentations throughout the course and put together a slide deck based on the reading of a research paper for that day. For these slides, we were assigned a role (like critic, entrepreneur, etc) to review the paper from. We were told we would have 2 assignments throughout the term but ended up having one that was due on the last day of classes. The assignment was interesting, as we got to see how to deform a 3D mesh and implement texturing through machine learning techniques.
Loved the class! Having a smaller class helped us for sure, but the presentation style isn't as daunting as it may seem. I started looking forward to presenting quite quickly as they allowed us to learn the topic more deeply than just attending a lecture (IMO). Mainly a paper reading class with discussions in class + a guest speaker. One coding assignment in the end was worth 30% of the grade and the rest was participation grading.
Very fun class! The one PSET for the whole class was very doable. The material is taught extremely well. I went from knowing only the basics about 3D Deep Learning, to knowing quite a bit! Definitely be sure to have taken the Pre-Reqs though. The focus of the teaching part of the class is on the 3D part, not really the deep learning part, so you should know that stuff like the back of your hand already.
Would recommend
