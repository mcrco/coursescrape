Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

Great class, strongly recommend.
This class provides a very hardware based, hands-on perspective on the systems engineering process behind space systems. If you are interested in sending something to space, this is a great class to do so!
It's a very good class and you learn a lot if you haven't really worked with electronics before, but do be ready to put in some time as some of the labs take a while to debug.
Interesting course if you're considering going into working on space missions. I definitely found listening to Andy's lectures interesting, but they were definitely a different style than typical Caltech lectures (more narrative, and more focused on students' input). The labs are a bit messy, but the class project, launching a high-altitude balloon with your own payloads, is definitely worth it. And Andy brings actual JPL higher-ups to the final design review, which was really cool (this class is probably also a good way to get connections).

I'd recommend against taking this class to learn about Arduino and other electronics. I know that there aren't many courses at Caltech that achieve that and that sucks, but you can easily learn that stuff on your own.
Overall decent course. There are weekly labs then the final project which involves you sending a high altitude balloon to space. Finally, the last part is a design review. The class structure is good but the weekly labs are a huge pain to complete because a lot of the boards simply were not working. I think if this issue was fixed the course becomes way better.
Very backloaded, but fulfilling to go through the launch. There are 6 labs, with the 6th one being the final launch. The labs build up to the launch, which is nice. The prior labs can be quite annoying sometimes since it's integrating deprecated Sparkfun modules with Arduino, and you soon learn the limitations of each of those. Good to learn, though! This year's launch was not as expected, but we still learned a lot from it. It was cool to experience!
From what I heard, lectures weren't all that relevant to the actual labs, but I wasn't able to go to any of the lectures anyway because it was scheduled during sports times. The professor has a day job at JPL so it's understandable (though very annoying because I really just wanted to go to lecture) that it's the only time that works for him, but even the organizational meeting was scheduled during 4-6 pm. Overall it didn't matter much afaik but if you're an athlete in season just be aware this might happen to you.

The labs are very much "here's a final product, make it" with not much help on the intermediate steps. Asking the ta does help but the labs always seemed to take a long time (even for the seniors/grad students) so the lab time with the ta runs out very quickly. This causes minor issues (especially if you're not very used to hardware debugging) to take a very long time to figure out which can get very tedious. The lab reports weren't worth the amount of effort I put in, though that may have been because the ta graded easily (thank you James). The balloon launch is fun so try to go to that if you can, and it's interesting to see the actual process of launching and collecting data.

Generally this class was ok. It gives good exposure to using the different sensors/arduino and interfacing with each other, and you will learn at least some practical skills from this. Plus the balloon is fun :) it felt a little disorganized though and was definitely a time sink at times - I don't think I enjoyed the class in the moment for a not-insignificant part of it. But an ok class overall that you could take if you don't have anything else
Overall, this is a cool class. You get to launch a HAB with several payloads at the end of the term. I haven't taken many other courses here that have a neat end project, so that's a plus. Prof. Klesh is very knowledgeable and really encourages students to engage critically with the course. He really treats us like we are colleagues working on the mission, rather than students.

The biggest issue I had with this course this year was the lack of organization and communication about course expectations. We did not receive timely feedback on lab assignments, and there was not enough support during lab sessions. This made it difficult to complete the assignments on time and ensure launch readiness.
