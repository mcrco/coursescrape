Comments

What would you tell a student thinking about taking this course in the future?

Lots & lots of work. Lots & lots of debugging. I didn't realize how much time this course would take when I signed up for it.
The units are definitely underloaded. The class requires a lot of time to finish since getting in-person help and troubleshooting on limited boards makes the assignments difficult.
SEVERELY UNDERUNITED. The first 5 homeworks weren't that bad, but after week 5 it really picks up. The last 5 homeworks are all programming assignments and you have to do them all in lab. I think as long as you don't fall behind, you will be fine. This is definitely the only class where I learned a lot frosh year and you'll be able to see your progress through each demo. I still recommend it even though it requires a lot of time and effort.
THIS IS A VERY FUN CLASS. You should certainly take it. By the end of the course you will have designed your own CPU from nothing but a few logic gates. Even if you are not an EE or CS major, I highly recommend. Yes, it is slightly underunited, but it really is not that bad at all(~8 hrs/week) until hw 9, which is when you design the control unit. However, even during week 9 if you just grind it out for 2 or 3 days you can finish pretty quickly, then hw 10 is extremely easy(2-3 hrs) and very rewarding as the CPU is completed afterwards. The satisfaction that you get from this class is like no other, and it is extremely rewarding, and this is likely a class you will not regret taking. Keep in mind, however, that all the times that I noted above were without doing the extra credit, which takes a bit longer. Also, deadlines don't really seem to exist in this class, so you don't have to worry about that.
Assignments in the second half of the course are super fun though that does make this course extremely backloaded. Be sure you can handle a 6 credit course taking well over 10 hours for every assignment past the halfway mark of this course.
DO NOT FALL BEHIND! All is okay and extremely rewarding if you stay on top of each week's homework assignment. I speed ran all my sets for other classes so I could budget out entire days for the subbasement, and I was usually able to finish by Friday night.
EE 10a is a great course, my favorite of this term. Lectures were very relevant to any EE field and Glen is a great lecturer and makes every lecture (in person and video) very understandable. As for the weekly labs/homeworks, they start off great and are very manageable (albeit with exception of ALU and CU), but make sure you spend a lot of time designing the control unit the correct way. Depending on your method of designing it, debugging can prove to be very tedious, a good word to describe the end of the course.
Takes a lot of time especially in the tail end of the course.
As an elective, this class is well worth it and super fun. ALU, Control Unit, and the Finite State Machine will take you a significant amount of time, so be prepared. Its very important to spent time thinking about how you will do your implementation of the control unit before you go about it. By far the hardest part of this class is the debugging, and if your not careful you can make your life a nightmare. Otherwise, its a Glen class with all the strings attached that you might expect there, but again well worth it. Definitely the class I enjoyed taking the most this term.
