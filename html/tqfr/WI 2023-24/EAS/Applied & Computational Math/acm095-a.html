ACM95a is an interesting course. I had to take it as part of MechE core, but to be honest, I'm really glad that I did. You probably know by now that the first half is dedicated to complex analysis, which most people (at least I) did not have a ton of background knowledge for, and then switches to ODEs, which you will be more familiar with from Ma2. As for complex analysis, it was definitely a bit tricky to try and grasp some of the more advanced concepts, but genuinely, it turned out to be really interesting. Even if you don't have an intuition for complex numbers, the class presents this material in a very clear and manageable way. I ended up really understanding and enjoying the complex analysis stuff, perhaps because it was a new field of math that I found pretty cool. The ODEs part of the course, like I said, will be more familiar, so hopefully that doesn't have too bad of a learning curve associated with it.

As for how to best learn in this course, I found it best to both go to lectures and review the lecture notes. Professor Hoffmann is a phenomenal professor and she really has a lot of enthusiasm about the material she is teaching. You can very easily walk out of these lectures feeling like you actually learned something new, and that is obviously pretty motivating. Going to lecture, for me at least, meant that I was keeping myself aware of what we were doing in class, and helped me understand the big picture of what was going on. In addition, the posted lectures notes for this class are top notch. They go through everything you need to know in tons of detail and present things in a very organized and coherent fashion. Reviewing these lecture notes after lectures really helped make sure that I was able to clear up any confusions I had, and the ability to read and revisit these were really key in helping me grasp the material. They were also extremely useful for the homework sets.

As for the workload, you have to remember that the class is listed as a 12 unit class for a reason. These problems required you to go through the lecture notes and really understand the content, and for me they took lots of thinking to try and figure out. Of course, that is super rewarding because as you think through the problems, you are actively learning by doing the homework, but that also means that it takes time. I would usually just block out my Saturday to work just on these sets, so that I could brainstorm and get it done and have the office hours in the evening to clear things up. So sets were rough, took time, but for a 12 unit course, that was expected.

All in all, I found this to definitely be a challenging course, but one that is really interesting and very well organized!
