This is definitely a great course to take! There are many MedE students in the class but I'm not one of them, so with little background in the field to start with, I found this introductory course a good point to have an overview on what medical devices include and how to design them.
Professor Gao organized the course really well such that all contents followed one another, and the delivery of the contents was really friendly for someone who has not been exposed to the field before. Professor Gao has been a great teacher of the course! There were also some guest lecturers invited who also followed the structure of the course well, so overall it was really enjoyable.
The workload was really fine with 3 homework sets in total. The last one was sent out a bit late so we had it due during the Finals week, but most contents in the sets are based on lecture slides, so with a bit of additional resources, I could finish them quite easily within 2-3 hours each. There was a Final presentation and a Final paper, but both demanded the same topic of our choice in a group. We spent quite some time on this (probably 10-15 hours in total for each member), but it was really a good chance for us to dig into an interested topic and do some research on it.
Feedback has not been very timely, but you would expect to get good marks from the course as long as you put into efforts.
