Comments

Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? In particular, comments about workload/distribution of the workload of the course, the necessity of the textbook, unexpected time requirements or flexibility, or unspecified prerequisites could be particularly helpful.

Don't procrastinate on your homework, you don't know what issues might pop up last minute
You complete one project throughout the term. You can choose among three options, with varying difficulty/time commitment.

There are four 1.5-2 hr lectures throughout the term. They will be in the evening, and the day is decided by vote at the OM. The course is possible without the lectures, for example if you have a conflict, but it is useful to go. You can also get caught up at the OH because the TAs are very knowledgeable and helpful.

There are four tutorials: schematic, PCB, thru-hole soldering, and surface mount soldering. Be prepared to add these to your schedule during specific weeks. The times are announced the week-of.

In finals week, you solder your PCB. You can only solder at OH, and OH get busy because there are only 8 soldering stations and 1 TA. Just be prepared for this.

Also be prepared that in week 10, you'll have one 1.5-hr lecture and two 2-hr soldering tutorials.

For grading, you get an A if your PCB works and you met the two milestones (turn in your schematic, turn in your PCB).
As a non-EE major, I think I gained a lot from this course. It was interesting to learn about how PCBs are fabricated and to go through the process of laying one out myself. Soldering is another useful skill that we were taught at the end of the class. The only thing I had hoped to understand better coming into the course was how the circuits in the PCBs worked.
I took this class because I wanted to learn more about EE. This is an awesome class and I highly recommend it. I am a CS major with no EE background and it was a pretty simple course. It goes over the entire process from designing the schematic to then actually building a board. The class makes it very simple to do each step. I took the class as a senior and I highly recommend for others take it. In terms of work, there are 3 assignments, submitting schematic, PCB, and soldering board. I would say schematic, PCB, and soldering each take about 6 hours.
EE13 is a fun class. You'll learn how to design a schematic, design a PCB, and solder a PCB. It's correctly united- and the TAs are super helpful. Take it if it's of interest!
Super fun! I'm not an EE but had no issues with doing the class. The class has essentially 3 lectures (which aren't super useful/necessary for the project), and several lab sections to learn how to use Diptrace and how to solder. Definitely go to office hours for help, and make sure to start the PCB early because you'll likely need to go through several rounds of revisions. I came out of the class really having enjoyed myself. I recommend to anyone who is interested in pcbs!
This is a useful and very practical class, and I would recommend it to anyone who is interested - any major. You'll learn the basics of schematic capture and PCB layout through one of three pre-made circuits designs (you choose which).

The class is very straightforward, so you just need to start each step as early as you can to leave yourself time to make revisions/debug. The time commitment averages to probably around 3 hours per week, but really there are some weeks where you might do nothing and others that will take a lot of time (depending on how you spread out your own workload).
