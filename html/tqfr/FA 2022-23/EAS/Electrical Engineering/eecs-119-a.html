Comments

Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? In particular, comments about workload/distribution of the workload of the course, the necessity of the textbook, unexpected time requirements or flexibility, or unspecified prerequisites could be particularly helpful.

The course is awesome for people who wants to learn Advanced Digital, VHDL, digital IC design etc stuff but be prepared for spending more than 15 hrs on few homeworks. The course is fully evaluated on homework sets. Out of which, initial 5 were easy and doable within time limit but the implementation homework (6 and 8) are quite difficult. It includes hardware implementation which is good and need to learn but also requires lots of effort. I personally spent more than 25 hrs on this two homeworks. Apart from this, Prof Glen is really good at this subject, he has very good knowledge about the digital design course work and Ray is also good and helpful as TA.
The workload for the first 5 weeks is reasonably light taking 4-6 hours a week. Starting with the more advanced VHDL coding assignments, the workload increases significantly but unlike EE/CS 10, I never found myself spending more than 10-12 hours on an assignment.
This course, like all Glen classes, is a hard but fulfilling ride. Like other students have mentioned, the first 5 homeworks were fairly reasonable and only slightly tedious at times. Homeworks 6 through 8, though, required significant VHDL implementation and testing and took me around 20-30 hours outside of class to finish. This was most likely because I was a complete newbie to VHDL and it took me a long time to get the hand of the syntax and design aspect. However, I can say that at the conclusion of this course, I feel I have learned an exponential amount about VHDL and gate array design. Glen and Ray were always available to help and answer questions, and I never felt as frustrated or lost in this class as I was in EE/CS 10, most likely because this class ramps up a little more slowly and prepares you well for the final assignments. And of course, the feeling of getting the last homework to work is like no other. So, I highly recommend this class if you want to learn digital design in VHDL.
Homeworks for the first 5 weeks took me maybe 4-8 hours, with a rough upward trend week-to-week in general. They were honestly kind of fun, kind of logic puzzle-y. If you took EE10a, they're a bit like the early assignments in that class.

Homeworks 6 and 8 were tough and took me at least 12 hours each. Homework 7 took maybe 8-10 hours. Discussing these with classmates and going to OH is really helpful. There are important pieces of information re: how to use EDA software to synthesize your designs that could potentially save you lots of time. Think very critically about how you plan to implement your design properly before actually writing the code -- it is not fun or easy to debug using waveforms.

I recommend trying to avoid taking too many extensions. They pile up and then you spend multiple days in a row just doing work for this class during finals week and that's no fun. Try to spread out the time you spend on this class, because un-frying your brain will often make you more efficient.

In terms of the quality of the content - if you enjoy thinking about digital logic design and synthesis, I recommend this class. Glen is a pretty good lecturer and very responsive to student feedback. Enjoy the ride!
Speaking as someone who's worked on/observed digital design in industry, I think this class is a really excellent introduction to making more complex designs.

I'm one of the slower students at Caltech, and I'm also a perfectionist, so I found this class quite time consuming. However, if you don't try to do everything on every assignment, the time commitment for this class can actually be really reasonable.

Beware hidden compiler optimization settings and other little details that people don't tell you about that can really screw you over - talk to others when things don't seem right.

Overall, I would highly recommend the class to anyone who is interested in digital design.
