Comments

Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? In particular, comments about workload/distribution of the workload of the course, the necessity of the textbook, unexpected time requirements or flexibility, or unspecified prerequisites could be particularly helpful.

The assignment difficulty was higher than the other classes I've taken at Caltech, but the TAs were very helpful with reinforcing our understanding on topics tested on the assignments.
The lectures were recorded, which is extremely helpful for going back and reviewing topics. The notes for the class were a "fill in" packet style, which was not as effective for me, but also trying to handwrite all the notes would be difficult as well. The due dates for assignments are relatively flexible. Review differential equations!
Course starts off very easy for the first few weeks, the rapidly picks up. Spend a couple of hours studying properties of derivatives, because a large portion of this course is just derivatives. I think this course is manageable for someone who had never taken any thermo before as you start from the beginning. I forgot all my thermo and managed to survive, so you probably can too!
Definitely use office hours and talk to other students about the products, as there are several correct ways to answer questions, some more intuitive than others! Also, be wary of using the textbook, as sometimes it can just confuse you more.
This class is very well taught and you will learn a good amount. I definitely recommend if you are interested in a thermo course.
This class is very highly organized; Prof. Minnich provides a very coherent set of lecture notes which we go over in class. Because of this there is very little outside effort students need to make in order to understand the material. I found some of the proofs of thermodynamic concepts to be a bit confusing - more intuitive than fully rigorous. However, Prof. Minnich is very welcoming of questions and cares about the students' understanding.
This class is helpful if you're entering with a shaky foundation in thermodynamics. The homework and assignments get everyone on the same page quickly and then expand in breadth.
The course follows the Callen textbook, and much homework is from it. There are some tough questions on the assignments, which require you to attend office hours. The exams were more conceptually complex than the homework.
Course is fine. No major complaints. The lectures are dull and the homeworks have huge variance in difficulty. Some are easy, others feel like you've been taught nothing of what's on it. The tests are fair although the time estimates given might not be the most accurate.
