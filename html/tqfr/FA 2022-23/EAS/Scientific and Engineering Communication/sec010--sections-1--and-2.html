Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? In particular, comments about workload/distribution of the workload of the course, the necessity of the textbook, unexpected time requirements or flexibility, or unspecified prerequisites could be particularly helpful.

This course is really good! It talks about many techniques to make good presentations and I learned how to use good vocal dynamics and make useful slide visuals after taking this class that will help me in SURF and future research presentations as well. Dr. Javier is a really caring person who does her best to make sure everyone understands the material. She tries to connect with students and is a very fair and understanding grader.

As for the work, there was usually one small quiz/writing assignment on Canvas per week. One ungraded presentations (an elevator pitch) at the beginning of the term and two graded presentations (a whiteboard talk and a standard Powerpoint presentation) for the midterm and final respectively. The grading is really fair and if you follow all the requirements you should get an A. It is a very low pressure class. I strongly recommend taking it.
The class does a great job helping you understand how to present technical material in an effective way, even if you have past experience in public speaking.
Accurately united course. I would recommend talking to Dr. Javier if you are ever unsure about how to enforce a strong central message throughout your talk. Dr. Javier also gives fantastic, specific feedback after every talk that helps you become a more confident, clear presenter.
If you are having trouble giving presentations, this course will be helpful to guide you as long as you pay attention to the material.
This course was a great change of pace from my more rigorous classes while also remaining informative. The class engagement was fun and I would recommend this course to anyone!
There is a personal story, an elevator pitch, an educational "chalk" talk, and a symposium. Topics on each can be whatever you want subject to some constraints regarding the goal of the presentation (to tell, to convince, to teach, and to whatever). It's not too much work and chill.
This is an enjoyable and useful class. We learned a number of specific ways to improve presentations (for content, delivery and slide design).
If you miss more than 3 days, then you automatically fail the class. You cannot attend lectures virtually either. You might be able to attend other sections?

Class material is somewhat helpful for teaching you presentation skills. It's more useful for being an outlet to "constantly" practice presenting to others.
You'll do a lot of public speaking/presenting practice, but because the class size is small and the professor is understanding, it shouldn't feel as daunting. Most weeks, you'll have the first two lecture days as actual lectures, and the last day to do homework asynchronously.
Dr. Javier is really nice and helpful and this class was perfectly painless even though I started out not so confident in my presentation skills. The assignments and smaller presentations leading up to the final presentation were fun and I feel like I got to learn a lot through my classmates' presentations too. :)
