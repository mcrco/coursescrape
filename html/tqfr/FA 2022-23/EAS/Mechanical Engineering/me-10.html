Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? In particular, comments about workload/distribution of the workload of the course, the necessity of the textbook, unexpected time requirements or flexibility, or unspecified prerequisites could be particularly helpful.

The workload is very light. Just remember to submit the quiz after each lecture where you write about what you found useful in the lecture and what you learned. These quizzes are the only assignments in this course. They are there to record attendance and to make sure the students attended and listened to the lectures.
I liked it. Very fun. Great way to understand ME path and potential career choices.
This course is really nice to sit through as a ME because you get too have a better idea of what you can expect in the workforce. Its also really great because it is so interactive and if you have questions about work opportunities you can always go up afterwards to speak to the presenters. They are all very kind and interested in helping you succeed.
Good course, very interesting speakers. You can learn both technical stuff like what classes are available as well as "life lessons" in engineering like decision making and academia vs. industry.
If someone is even considering ME as a major this class will definitely help people decide if it is for them as it offers a wide variety of experiences which help people also learn which part of ME they like. I found that I liked the industry section the most out of the class.
This class was exactly what you would expect. Just a one hour lecture every week (45 minlecture, 15 min to eat pizza and ask questions). if you missed a lecture the organizing prof required you to make it up.
Recommend this class. The lecturers gave valuable life lessons, not just information pertaining to engineering. Some of them also provided insight into major life decisions like academia vs. industry, small company vs. big company, etc. Overall lectures were always very interesting.
This was one of my favourite courses. You'll learn about some of the research being done by profs at Caltech, some of the courses you'll take if you become a MechE major and have the opportunity to learn about the applications of engineering in industry, academia, entrepreneurship, sustainability and more. If you have any questions for the presenters after their lectures, you should definitely approach them! They're super kind and love talking about their work. If you have any questions about majoring in MechE, you can consult Prof. Colonius. He's very approachable.
