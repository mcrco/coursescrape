ME8 is an extremely interesting class that is a must if you're at all interested in robotics. The course content is well designed and gives a great perspective on fundamentals. Some experience with object-oriented programming is probably recommended, but you can learn quickly even if you don't have any.

Overall, I'd definitely recommend ME8 to people interested in robotics - not necessarily just MechE majors either! It was easily my most fun class this term.
I would recommend looking into Python and having some coding experience for this class. I had coding experience prior to this class but not in Python. This made some of the labs harder, but I was still able to do very well in the class. My experience would be better if I knew Python beforehand or went to the Python coding review session at the beginning of the year.
class structure: one lecture a week. goals to work through every week in lab (completed in teams of two). flexible (and plenty of) oh.

my experience: i loved the class. i would highly recommend people take this for their intro lab requirement. i enjoyed having a tangible end-product. the goals most weeks were very reasonable, except for maybe week 2, week 6, and week 8. but there's plenty of oh, and the tas are very helpful! there was also technically flexible deadlines. be sure to choose a good partner though; it is very easy to have unequal contribution, which can be a little frustrating. prof gunter is also simply excellent, and is inreadibly resourceful.
i loved this class so much!!! it was so well done and the labs were so much fun (huge relief after ch3x) i would recommend this class to anyone who wants to do another intro lab and gunter is amazing!! i wish the tas were more aware of the lab so they can be more helpful but gunter was very understanding!!
Really good class. I took it because it counts as an additional lab and it was worth it. At the start, it was pretty easy, but at some point, it got hard but manageable. I would really recommend office hours and meeting with the professor.
Good class to take for additional lab requirement. As a CS major, it's nice to see real life applications. Amount of lab time varied each week depending on the lab. Definitely increased towards the end of term but overall, great class.
This course was very fun! It's a great introduction to robotics. I would say that even if you already know a bit about robotics (I did) this class is very rewarding.

We designed and 3D printed parts for the robot which was very cool! We then programmed raspberry pis and used open cv to make a gimbal follow a ball. Professor Niemeyer is a very good lecturer and answers questions very well. He is so passionate about his work that it's contagious. I had a lot of fun doing labs.

I did end up backloading the work. I did the first 3-4 weeks worth of work on time but then I sort of got very busy with other classes so I basically did 2-3 weeks of ME 8 during finals week. It was difficult to squeeze the work but it was doable. I think I would think of this as a lower effort frosh lab than other labs (note that I am a junior).

If there's anything that could be complained about was that the sets took a while to be released. However, I presume that that is because they were using a new environment and all the sets were being designed from scratch - they also extended the deadlines accordingly. Overall, this is a good fun rewarding frosh lab.

It was a great class. You create a robot from scratch which is super cool. You 3D print, assemble the robot, and use opencv. Great intro lab and I learned a lot. Also it being on p/f is a huge plus. You have to finish all the labs, but you can take your time and don't need to worry about perfecting things.
This was probably my favorite course this term. The purpose of this course was to construct a robot camera that would swivel in two directions and track objects. The steps to complete the robot were broken down into tasks for each week. The amount of time the weekly assignments took varied a lot, but overall I thought it was pretty reasonable. Also, the way assignments were turned in was you would write a very short report on what you had done that week, accompanied by a few screenshots. We were told that if the homeworks took more than 6 hours it was acceptable to write that we did not have enough time to finish that week. I would keep in mind that this class requires you to work with a partner throughout the entire term, which adds a lot of variability to your experience, especially if you take it as a first term freshman. It seemed like people's experiences varied a lot depending on how much programming experience they and their partner had and whether they worked well with each other.
Pretty easy class, even for pass fail. By the end I actually felt really accomplished with what I'd learned. Having a working robot that could look around and track different objects felt really cool. If you're interested in robotics at all it's definitely a class worth taking. One complaint I'd have is that Gunter (the professor) had a habit of releasing the sets kinda late. I think this class is sort of in its infancy so I would hope that's an issue that gets sorted out later in the course.
