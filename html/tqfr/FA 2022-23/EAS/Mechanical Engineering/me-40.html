I really liked ME 40. I found the course material to be very interesting and very useful. I'm 100% sure I will make extensive use of more than one, if not all, of the topics discussed at some point in my career. In terms of difficulty, I would say it is very manageable. It was stressful at times when combined with other difficult courses such as ME 72a, but overall very manageable. That being said, there were a few brutal problem sets interspersed throughout the term. With regards to the format of the sets, it was usually 1-2 written problems along with 1-2 Matlab problems. I feel like this was a good balance. In addition, there was no midterm exam, but there is a final. I think that Professor Blanquart did a very good job of presenting the material in an informative way and I found the lectures to be engaging. I would recommend attending them as much as possible.
ME 40 is pretty fair overall. The sets were manageable with an occasional question or two to piazza or an office hour. The lecture slides are very well made and clearly outline the information that is important.
I'm not sure what I was supposed to learn in this course. All of the content beyond week 2 was already covered in Ma3 and ACM95b. I spent 10-15 hours per week mindlessly debugging matlab code. It's probably worth pushing this course off until after ACM11 so you can be more familiar with matlab when you take this course.
I'm not really sure WHY this class is required for MEs, this class is basically just watered down Ma3 plus some fourier stuff that we already saw in ACM95 plus some dimensional analysis. I really would have benefited more from a more "normal" statistics class or anything more relevant to the major with more specific applications.
This class feels like a hodgepodge of content someone decided we should learn without stopping to think if we needed to learn it. As a result, the class has no central idea other than increasingly more time-consuming MATLAB assignments.

The way extensions work in this class is extremely unclear because Guillaume never responds to emails :'(
I thought I would enjoy this class since I generally liked having Guillaume as a professor for 11a/b and because statistics was my favorite class in high school. Unfortunately, I didn't find the topics presented in this class that interesting or useful, and my opinion of Guillaume has been significantly soured.

The course started off with some dimensional analysis, which doesn't seem all that useful and was fairly confusing and arbitrary. Then we moved on to some probability and statistics/regression, which was alright but would have been better had it been taught correctly. Instead we were taught to apply a confidence interval in the completely backwards and wrong way. We finished up with some more regression techniques for multivariate data and then Fourier series, which was probably the best part of the class.

In previous years I heard this class was pretty backloaded, but to Guillaume's credit I would say he paced it pretty well. The sets were mostly of a reasonable length, but the annoying part is that they use Matlab pretty heavily but we weren't allowed to use certain Matlab functions, and it was rarely clear what we were allowed to do or not.

Last year, Guillaume struck me as a very kind professor who was always looking out for our well being, but that didn't seem to carry over to this year. I'm not sure what his objective was with the constant snide remarks and occasional full on rants about us spending time on ME 72, but it made his lectures even less useful and enjoyable and it wasn't too effective in increasing attendance. He had some other interesting moves like making a set due on Black Friday, ignoring all protests until extending the deadline at the last minute, then refusing to answer questions about the set after the original deadline had passed.
The course covered 4 main topics: dimensional analysis, probability and statistics, regressions, and Fourier series and transforms. Each topic took about 2 weeks.

By the time you have taken this class, you have probably taken ACM 95ab and maybe Ma 3, so you will know about 75% of what's covered in the class. If you haven't taken these classes, you will struggle because Guillaume's lectures and lecture notes are mostly useless at explaining the material. About half of his lecture time is grinding through mathematical derivations without justifying most of the steps or assumptions, so you will not gain any conceptual understanding. If you have taken ACM 95b and Ma 3, you will realize that the lectures have lots of mistakes and many assumptions that are never mentioned or justified. If you show Guillaume's lectures to Professors Meiron or Cvitanic, they would be appalled.

Most homework assignments require a level of understanding much more advanced than you could ever gain from the lectures alone. For the homework on probability, statistics, regressions, and Fourier series/transforms, I would recommend you forget about Guillaume's lectures and find lecture notes from ACM 95b or Ma 3. For the homework on dimensional analysis, find some good textbooks that have a section on dimensional analysis and read them. They will teach you proper methods for dimensional analysis that Guillaume never covers in class. The TAs will nitpick your solutions (especially your MATLAB plots) to find reasons to take points off so check your work several times before submitting it.

The homework assignments usually take well over 6 hours per week, especially if you "write down your assumptions and thought processes," as every homework says at the top of the page. Guillaume would never know this because he puts very little effort into solving his own homework. The homework 2 solutions were just a copy of his ME 11c lecture slides from 2020. At least 2 of the posted homework solutions included problems copied directly from last year's ME 40. Several other posted homework solutions are missing some problems. Every solution that Guillaume wrote by himself does not include his "assumptions and thought processes." Most of the posted MATLAB solutions do not format the plots neatly. Some plots have legends that block almost all of the data and others have markers that are so big that the plots just look like blue blobs. If the TAs graded Guillaume's posted homework solutions the way they would grade yours, Guillaume would not get an A in his own class.

Towards the end of his final review session at the end of term, Guillaume insulted Professor Shepherd's ME 11c. I find this unacceptable. Dr. Shepherd had useful lectures and lecture notes, detailed homework solutions, and fair grading procedures, none of which Guillaume had.
-Lecture pace was fine, but the treatment of topics was rarely mathematically precise. Teaching was done more with pictures, ideas, and "proofs"

-I heard some peers remark on this point, so I'll say it as well: it was upsetting to hear the professor call out other professors for being inadequate at instructing. This is unprofessional and sets a bad tone for the lecture.

This was an uninspiring, shallow treatment of several deep and interesting topics. Frankly, everything that is taught in this course can be learned in other courses (Ma3 + ACM104 + ACM95b), so I hope the department reconsiders the purpose of having this course at all or at least redesigns it to be more interesting through cool data analysis projects or something.

Big Yikes. Not sure why this course exists, and especially not sure why we are required to take it. There were 4 main topics of the class: dimensional analysis, statistics, and I forget the other two. We started with dimensional analysis, which was extremely confusing, and I never understood what the purpose was of either of the two methods that we learned. The two weeks we spent on statistics were a complete repeat (but more confusingly taught) or what we learned in Math 3. Then we spent some time doing Series stuff, which was a complete repeat of concepts from ACM 95. ACM 95 is required for the major already, and Math 3 while not required is taken by most students in the major. So, not sure why we needed to learn math (that we already knew) from a MechE professor.

Biggest problem with this course though was the attitude of the professor throughout the term. Every lecture, Blanquart spent at least a few minutes berating/shaming us for being in ME 72, and for spending time on ME 72 that we could have instead spent on his class. He repeatedly told us that he looked forward to seeing us fail in the ME 72 mobility demo. He also threw an absolute fit about the low attendance in lecture. At the beginning of the course, he made it clear that it was okay to watch the recordings, and that we could still get attendance credit (25% of the overall grade) for watching recordings instead of attending in person. Then, a few weeks into term, after some days of very low attendance (about 10 students), he sent an email to the class saying he would no longer post recordings, and that in person attendance was mandatory. Then, three days later, he instantly walked that policy back, apologized for being mean, and let people watch recordings again. He still complained in class though about low attendance. Annoyingly, all of his complaints about low attendance were only heard by those of us who were in attendance to hear them (ironic).
