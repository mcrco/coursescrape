Start early, go to lecture, go to office hours.
This course is tough, but its fun. You learn a lot of cool things. Everyone will say its a lot of stress. This is only the case when you don't give yourself enough time. Just start the sets as soon as they come out. This will save you so much time.
Adam is one of the best teachers you'll ever have at Caltech, and this course is no exception. They're extremely invested in effective teaching, in engaging students, and in giving assignments that feel genuinely fulfilling to have done. I'm very grateful to have had the opportunity to take this class.

As for practical matters: This is a low-level C and Assembly course, dealing with topics like compilers, virtual machines, the kernel, memory, and so on. Capacity with the material of CS 3 (conceptual, coding, and debugging) is a must.

There's a textbook but it's free and provided to you online. The lectures are technically optional, but I think you get a lot out of attending (and you also get late tokens for it, which let you turn in assignments late).

There's ~1 project per week, no labs or smaller assignments, along with a midterm and a final. They vary in time commitment, but are usually somewhere between 6-12 hours each. However, they're fun! Mostly just coding, the occasional open-ended conceptual question but not for nearly as many points. The coding grades are done by automated tests, so you always know where you stand. (This *is* the sort of course that argues that code which doesn't work is worth nothing, so a project that's *almost* correct still gets a zero. Don't start them the day that they're due!)
