Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? In particular, comments about workload/distribution of the workload of the course, the necessity of the textbook, unexpected time requirements or flexibility, or unspecified prerequisites could be particularly helpful.

The workload is not so high even without background of coding and machine learning.
This class is a mixed bag.

On one hand, it is a very consistent amount of work per week, which is nice because it fits well into your schedule. The final is equivalent to a non-collab double set, and you are given two weeks to do it. It fits nicely into a schedule where you are taking another class that is very time consuming/difficult.

On the other, I don't feel like I come out of this class knowing how to actually implement a ML algorithm or use available tools online. To be fair, this is a part of a larger curriculum, involving CS 155 and CS 156B. While it teaches the inner workings of machine learning very well, this is very much a theoretical class (even if some of the problems are coding/implementation).

You should definitely be forewarned that the professor is very rigid. Yaser is an excellent lecturer; he is very good at intonation, and the lectures are interesting and entertaining. However, as he outlines at the beginning of the class, there are (basically) no extensions given out, and it is mandatory that you attend 8 (out of 18) lectures to pass the class. To be fair, there are other policies that boost your score.
