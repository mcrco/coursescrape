Comments

Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? In particular, comments about workload/distribution of the workload of the course, the necessity of the textbook, unexpected time requirements or flexibility, or unspecified prerequisites could be particularly helpful.

I feel like anyone who's done elementary probability and real analysis covered in a typical mathematics undergraduate course should be well prepared for this course.
Would definitely recommend taking real analysis (Ma 108a) before this class, and being familiar with some functional analysis (CMS 107) also helps. If you have taken Ma 108b and did not get the point of measure theory, this class is perfect for you (or at least it was for me). Coming into the class, I thought measure theory was awful, and now I think it's pretty cool (at least when it's used for probability). The homework took on average around 11 hours, some more and some less, and one cool thing that happens is that you solve some seemingly arbitrary theoretical problem for homework but then that thing you proved comes back in the context of probability as something really crucial to the theory.
As currently done, the workload is higher than the credits, please be advised. But very worthwhile and helpful, if you have time.
problem sets due on Monday made the office hours not useful since they were at the end of the week. I and many others do homework on the weekend so I was never able to get help in office hours.
The material covered in the course was great - it was my first time learning measure theory and the content was always related back to elementary concepts so we could compare the modern formal interpretation with the more heuristic ideas in probability theory. The provided course notes are essentially a textbook plus/minus some things that were being updated over the duration of the course and are quite thorough. The homeworks were reasonable length and informative.

Joel is also a great instructor. He's very detail-oriented, almost always knows how to answer your question instantly, and paces the class well. He's also very responsive outside of class, especially on Piazza.

Analysis and linear algebra help a lot for the course, though you don't need to be extremely fluent to get the main ideas.
Great course. Three 1.5 hr classes per week plus homework is a lot. But Prof made the class fun and all the materials are well organized. There are lecture notes, recordings, whiteboard pics, piazza, and OHs - felt well-supported.
This class is a lot of work. If possible, particularly for those graduate students concurrently in ACM 122 and/or ACM 107, I would delay this class for the second year. It builds on several concepts in ACM 107 (making them perhaps overwhelming to do at the same time) and I felt like I was able to gain more from the class by not taking them all at the same time.
The workload is more than what's stated on the syllabus. Expect to work really hard but you will learn a lot. I probably spent 24+ hours on every homework. Keep in mind though, I did not have a very good background in probability theory / pure math before taking this course. I also wanted to finish and understand all the HW questions very well. The course is definitely difficult, particularly in the 2nd half. But the professor and the TAs are excellent!
I really enjoyed the class. Prof. Tropp was super clear in lectures, and provided valuable framing (how to think about the topics, how do they relate to each other, what topics were more important, etc.).

The lecture notes matched up well with the lectures, which made it easier to review material after class because I didn't need to spend effort looking for and piecing together the relevant material, it was already all in one place.
