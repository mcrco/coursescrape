Unlike any other linear algebra class I have ever experienced, ACM104 has actually helped me retain what I learn and develop problem solving skills beyond rote memorization. I really did not resonate with Ma1b and forgot most of its coverage, but ACM104 has made me love linear algebra.

ACM104 runs deeply through the fundamentals of linear algebra while also covering applications to a plethora of fun bonus concepts beyond a typical linear algebra class, including Google's PageRank algorithm, facial recognition, image compression, k-means clustering, the power method, principal component analysis (PCA), graph partitioning and more!

Furthermore, ACM104 includes MATLAB problems that can improve your skills with matrix manipulation and linear systems in MATLAB, as well solidify an intuition for the different algorithms and processes in numerical analysis covered in the class (including working with polynomial interpolation, Legendre polynomials, Gram matrices, Gram-Schmidt, some machine learning methods like PCA and k-means, etc.).

Professor Zuev's lecture notes were always captivating to read and self-contained, and Pavlos's review sessions and OH were incredibly helpful.

Strongly recommend this class!
