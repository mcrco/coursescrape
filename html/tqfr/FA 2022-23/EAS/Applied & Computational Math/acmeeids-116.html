Going into this class, I was scared of not following along because Ma3 flew by so fast and I felt it did not solidify concepts enough.

But, like all Zuev classes, this class is truly one of a kind and can help you feel much better about your understanding of the topic at hand. ACM116 will solidify your understanding of probability and statistics, as well as familiarize you with different kinds of transformations and introduce you to stochastic processes including Poisson processes, Markov chains, and Brownian motion. The lecture notes are extremely comprehensive and follow along a good pace with the sets, often including captivating real-life examples of applications that make it easy to visualize the processes occurring.

For the coding aspect of the class, I think it is very important to have some MATLAB experience (ACM11, ACM104, or equivalent) prior to enrolling in this class because the MATLAB problems on the sets can be quite involved. However, it is certainly not the majority of the class so if you have no options for gaining MATLAB experience prior just be sure to go to OH if you get stuck and/or find a good collab group.

Overall, this class is arguably the most important ACM class (along with Zuev's ACM104) for any career remotely attached to applied math. The probabilistic thinking skills you develop in this class can help a lot with both career interviews and in research.

TL;DR 11/10, great class, great professor, useful for virtually every field of interest, definitely consider taking it if you have space in your schedule
