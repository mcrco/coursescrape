This is a required class for many. I don't know what other iterations of the class were like with other professors, but the book by Sahakian and Helliwell is excellent if you find that the material is not great or coherent for the class when taught by other professors at Caltech.
I personally wasn't super excited by the content of 106a, but Professor Sahakian is a great instructor and lectures were always entertaining as well as informative. The textbook we used this year was clearly written and sufficient for solving the homework problems. The exams were about as difficult as the sets, and the sets were never unreasonably difficult/tedious. I would recommend this course to anyone who wants a stronger foundation in classical mechanics, as it is very well-taught.
Besides the description of Lagrangian and Hamiltonian formulations of mechanics there is a section on E&M that while not major included a chapter in the textbook and a single weeks set. This course also discusses symmetries and their relations to conservation laws (Noether's Theorem). Contents of ph 12C not expected but ph 12A and ph 12B very helpful
This was a great class! Despite the previous years' tqfrs and Caltech tradition, this class was very well taught and organized. In fact, I think this was one of the best taught physics classes I have taken so far and it gave me insight in physics beyond classical mechanics.
Sahakian is a fantastic instructor. He clearly cares not only about the course material, but about the students. His lectures are informative, well-paced, and lighthearted. Unfortunately, in the second half of the term, less well-taught classes (*cough* Ph125a with Frank Porter *cough*) sucked up so much of my time that I ended up reading the book for this class instead of making it to lecture. Fortunately, Sahakian's book is as good as his lectures! Even if he stops teaching this class in the future, I believe Helliwell and Sahakian's Modern Classical Mechanics will remain the best way to learn the content in Ph106a. Professor Sahakian is a shining example of a good college physics professor, and I consider myself lucky to have finally experienced such high-quality education after two full years at this godsforsaken place.
I try my best to not give out perfect scores on TQFRs but there really wasn't a fault in this class!

This class will teach lots of Lagrangian mechanics and the Hamiltonian formulation. Sets are 6 questions per week but you get to skip one of them. Homework 60% of grade, midterm 20%, final exam 20%. The exams are fair, and Alvin Li (one of the TAs for this course) was incredible. He not only hosted review sessions but also wrote his own version of the lecture notes. It was essentially a textbook! It was ~100 pages long and was an essential tool in reviewing for the final exam. He is one of the most dedicated TAs I have ever met and is very professional; if there was something he didn't know, he would say that he didn't know the answer and would get back to us later.
