This is a really interesting class- it was excellently taught (Professor Zmuidzinas is an awesome lecturer and great explainer- and was very easy to contact for questions). However it was also super challenging.

The homeworks took noticeably long to do- some taking over 12 hours, staying up to 5 am 3 nights in a row! The TAs were not super helpful either with respect to the homework.

Overall it's a good class- I'm not a Physics major so I took this for interest and got what I wanted out of it- but be prepared to work *a lot*. It's fast paced and it sometimes feels like drinking from a fire hose- especially towards the end with diffraction which was super unintuitive for a lot of us.
This course was interesting but definitely under-united. I strongly encourage working on sets in a group. The course emphasized mathematical understanding and problem-solving over conceptual understanding of the physics, which can be good or bad depending on the student. Sometimes an entire problem on the set would be dedicated to deriving mathematical results. While it was time-consuming and difficult, I think this course taught me a lot about how to use math when solving problems, and I gained a fair understanding of the physics of waves.
I really enjoyed the material in the class. Waves are very interesting, and I thought that Prof Zmuidzinas did a very good job (at least according to the lecture notes) of building up the concept of waves from base mathematical and physical principles.

As for the work, the first few problem sets felt a little bit excessive. They weren't particularly difficult but had a lot of nasty computations that didn't feel too enlightening in the end. After the third problem set, though, they became much fairer (until perhaps the very end of class) and did a better job of illuminating the material we were learning.

The midterm and final were worth a lot of the grade (40/25/35) in this class, and the midterm was pretty brutal as far as time goes. The final was very fair, though.

Overall, I'd recommend the class. Well taught, interesting material, and a relatively fair workload.
Prof. Zmuidzinas' teaching is excellent and has immaculate lecture notes every week. However, the problem sets are really long and take forever to finish. The first three weeks' problem sets made me extremely desperate, but they got slightly better after we provided the feedbacks. I personally think the biggest problem for this class was how bad the TAs were. They were the most unhelpful TAs I have ever had who simply don't care about the class and a lot of times don't know what's going on. So with all those incredibly difficult problems our resources for help were simply the few minutes after lectures where Prof. Zmuidzinas answers our questions. They might change the prof next year so everything might change again.
Course content was a little bit disjoint. It was not abundantly clear how the topics related to each other. Everything was being presented at a breakneck pace so there was little time to go back and reinforce each topic. The lecture notes were excellent and very helpful, they served as the main reference point for homework assignments since there was no one textbook being taught from. Homework was very challenging and lengthy, it took a long time to do. Many of the questions left me feeling that I didn't understand the material well. It was slightly unclear what exactly the question was asking for, and the answers I would end up with were very messy and not obviously correct.
