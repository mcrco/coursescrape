The workload is quite tedious but always doable. Timing out the set is always important and attending Sergi's office hours. The set can be done the day before but is not advisable.
Professor Hildebrandt is an amazing professor, and I sincerely hope he continues to teach Ph2a. This was a great course on waves for the general audience that got the idea across without being too technical or too boring/hand-wavey. Would take this class again.
I'd heard a lot of bad things about this class from people who had taken it in previous years with a different instructor, so I came in expecting a challenging and frustrating experience, but this class was not like that at all. Sergi is easily the most understanding and compassionate professor I've had, from bringing cookies and drinks to class to holding his very helpful office hours late at night to writing up a lengthy Piazza post to help clarify the questions on the final. Lectures were fun with weekly demos.

As for the course content, I thought it was laid out and paced pretty well while still covering everything important. The first half of the term looked at harmonic oscillators and RLC circuits while the second half dealt with the wave equation, diffraction, and dispersion. Having taken Math 2, ACM 95 and ME 12c helped me quite a bit with understanding the topics presented.

Overall this was a pretty enjoyable and useful class and I would definitely recommend taking it as long as Sergi is teaching it.
This course was significantly easier and more enjoyable than previous years. The exams were very reasonable (unlimited time, same difficulty as homework), and you could even retake the first midterm if you were not happy with your score. Sergi would give a bunch of hints on the exams, so you could easily check your answers/know if you were going in the right direction. He genuinely really cared about if you were learning and would even bring drinks and snacks to lecture for his students. Even though I am quite bad at physics, this was a great class, and I learned a lot.

Prof. Hildebrandt is an amazing lecturer, and he genuinely cares about students and actively incorporates student feedback. However, if you expect to apply Phys 2a knowledge in the future, I would choose Phys 12a instead. Up to week 4, the class was pretty well-structured; I felt that I was learning and understanding new concepts, and the HW was reasonably challenging. But after week 4, this class became extremely easy -- we had sets that included copying derivations off the lecture slides, summarizing articles online, applying a formula a bunch of times, etc, and our exams had pages of "hints" describing exactly how to solve each problem. Hopefully this changes in the future, but if not, I would only take Ph 2a if you're looking for a low effort, easy-A class. For any major that requires knowledge in Ph 2a, definitely take 12a instead.
