Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? The analytical track with Professor Hutchcroft is very good. It's all the fun of seeing how theorems are derived without the obstacles of confusing math jargon. He has a sense of humor, presents concepts as a sort of narrative, gives motivating proofs, and shares cool examples.
Homework is good practice for a real analysis class, living up to the name of the class. Not great practice for doing differential equations, although you do learn how to do some. In general, the notes were very sufficient to do the homework. Taking 108 is not necessary, but pretty helpful especially at the beginning of the class.
Despite hearing a lot of horror stories about the class from previous years, I thought math 2 analytical was a pretty good class this year. Surprisingly, as opposed to most math classes I have taken here, it actually got easier after the midterm, but I don't know if that was a reflection of midterm feedback or just the content being taught. Overall, I felt the grading was fair, although I don't know what the final curve will look like. My biggest gripe with this class is it felt like the most interesting and important fundamental results were still a bit out of reach, so much of the course work was just applying those, which was not that fun, although definitely easier than if we had to wrangle with the deeper side of the material. Still, I think this is probably just necessary considering the relatively low level of experience prerequisite to the class.

Definitely would recommend taking if you are a math major, as I have heard the practical track is still a lot of work, so this is probably better for learning about math you actually care about and improving proof-writing abilities.
This course is interesting, and Prof. Hutchcroft explains concepts well. This year the recitation was cancelled right before classes started, which probably made the course more difficult, but maybe there will be recitations in the future. There's a mix of proofs and practical problem-solving, but if you're not interested in the proofs behind differential equations, there's probably no need to take the analytical over the practical track.
The course is pretty tough. Make sure to read all the lecture notes and attend all the office hours.
I would definitely recommend this class to anyone who likes pure math, especially anyone who likes real analysis! Many of the topics were very new and interesting to me (asymptotic analysis! generating functions!), and the self-contained lecture notes made studying efficient. Be aware, though, that the sets only become harder! If you struggle at the beginning, it might be a good idea to switch to the practical track.

Going to the TA's office hours was helpful, but on busy days it was difficult for me to find time to think through the new ideas before the set was due (the office hours were only a few hours before the deadline). The professor's office hours weren't helpful for the homework, because they were often before the next homework problems had been released. In general, the course was not very well-organized because it was being taught for the first time by Professor Hutchcroft, but I'm sure that will improve.
Don't be scared by the first few weeks of the course! It will be confusing as you wouldn't have the expected analysis background for the first few weeks unless you've already taken Ma 108 or 110, but I promise it does get more comprehensible over time.
Ma2 (Analytical) is an infamous course. It's known to be one of the very unnecessary choices a Caltech student can make. It doesn't show up on your transcript, and you won't be rewarded in anyway other than what you take out of the class. Historically, it's been a very difficult and conceptually difficult class as well. But I decided to take the class anyways.

Prof. Hutchcroft is an amazing professor though. For the few lectures I attended they ended up being well spoken and intuitive. He hit a lot of key points and gave incredible and inutitive (yet quick) proofs. He's also incredibly compromising, giving class extensions for when things were a bit disorganized/chaotic during COVID times. The only qualm I had were that his notes were a first draft, filled with quite a bit of typos and will certainly be streamlined for next year. In a way I'll be a bit jealous for the people who get to take this course next year.

If you like Ma1a, definitely take this course if Hutchcroft is teaching. But even if you don't, it's something to consider.
