Do you have any constructive comments for other students considering taking this course? In particular, comments about workload/distribution of the workload of the course, the necessity of the textbook, unexpected time requirements or flexibility, or unspecified prerequisites could be particularly helpful.

In most of the presentations, the content was very hard to follow, and it was very easy to get lost right away without a good math background.
Not very useful for non-Math majors, and the professors who explained their research did not seem very passionate or make an effort to actually make the content understandable to freshman, so it wasn't particularly enjoyable either.
Some topics were completely over my head, and some were interesting (though still didn't make much sense). Don't expect to understand the majority of what the speakers are saying unless you have a pretty robust pure math background. There weren't really any applied math speakers, which was partially what I expected when signing up for this. But if you are interested in doing pure math, or just interested in getting exposed to pure math topics, I would take it anyway since it's a fairly low time commitment.
This class was great opportunity to learn about different fields of math research, and I looked forward to it every week! Some of the talks were hard to follow, but I would recommend staying to the end of the course, since there will be talks that you understand and learn a lot from. You get to learn about interesting stuff and only need to show up, so why not take this course? There is only one reason I can think of: this pizza course did not serve food. Hopefully this will change next year, but the lack of lunch could have made it difficult to stay in this course with some schedules.
Some lecturers presented topics that did not require to much (or any) background and explained them clearly, but other presentations I could scarcely understand because of the new terminology/concepts that they had to introduce as they chose topics that were (in my opinion) not suitable for most freshmen.
